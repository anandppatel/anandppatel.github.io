1.3. The orbit closure problem

To apply the method of undetermined coefficients, we have to ensure that no fibers of our family lie in the orbit closure of generic cubic surfaces (otherwise, we get excess intersection). Thus, we have to prove statements of the form ``a given cubic surface $S$ is not in the closure of a suitably generic cubic surface.'' This is, in general, a difficult problem. We develop a range of tools to address problems of this kind. To preserve the flow of the paper, and because these techniques may be of independent interest, we assemble these tools in a separate section (Section 5).
