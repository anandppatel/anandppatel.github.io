Proof. (0CAF)

The proof follows easily from unravelling the map $$ j \colon \widetilde {\mathcal Z}_e \to \F_1,$$ which we now do. Set $Z = \widetilde{\mathcal Z}_e$ and let $N_e$ be the 1-dimensional vector space $$ N_e = N_{E_i/\widetilde{S^{[2]}}} \big|_e. $$ From the proof of Proposition 3.13 [445B], we write the $\F_1$ component more canonically as $$ \F_1 = \P\left(N_e \otimes \O_{L_i} \oplus N_{L_i/S}\right). $$ The point $e \in E_i$ gives a (non-zero) map $$ d \beta \colon N_e \to N_{L^{[2]}_i / S^{[2]}}|_{\beta(e)}. $$ The point $\beta(e) \in L^{[2]}_i \subset S^{[2]}$ corresponds to the length 2 subscheme $Z \subset L_i \subset S$. It is easy to see that we have a canonical identification $$ N_{L^{[2]}_i / S^{[2]}}\big |_{\beta(e)} = H^0\left(Z, N_{L_i/S}|_Z\right). $$ We thus get a map $$ d \beta \colon N_e \to H^0\left(Z, N_{L_i/S}|_Z\right). $$ We leave it to the reader to verify that the map $j \colon Z \to \F_1$ is induced by the map $$N_e \otimes \O_Z \to N_e \otimes \O_Z \oplus N_{L_i/S}|_Z$$ given by $(\id, \epsilon \circ d\beta)$, where $$ \epsilon \colon H^0\left(Z, N_{L_i/S}|_Z\right) \otimes \O_Z \to N_{L_i/S}|_Z $$ is the evaluation map. Since $\epsilon$ is an isomorphism and $d \beta$ is non-zero, the second coordinate of (3.18) is non-zero. This non-vanishing equivalent to the proposition. (Incidentally, note that the first coordinate of (3.18) is invertible. This is equivalent to the fact that $Z \subset \F_1$ is disjoint from the curve where $\F_1$ meets the other component $S$. In the proof of Proposition 3.13 [445B], we concluded this fact using intersection numbers.)

The assertion about admissibility is straightforward.
