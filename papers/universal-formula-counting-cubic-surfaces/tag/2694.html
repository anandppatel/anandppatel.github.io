Proof. (2694)

By excision and homotopy invariance of equivariant Chow groups, we have $$ A^4(\mathscr M) = A^4_{\GL V}(W) = A^4_{\GL V}(\bullet).$$ The ring $A^*_{\GL V}(\bullet)$ is generated by the classes $v_i$, and hence $A^4_{\GL V}(\bullet)$ is a free $\Z$-module generated by the monomials in $v_i$ of total degree 4. Therefore, for every cubic surface $X$, we have an expression $$[\Orb(X)] = a_{1^4}v_1^4 + a_{1^2\cdot 2} v_1^2v_2 + a_{1\cdot 3} v_1v_3 + a_{2^2}v_2^2 + a_4 v_4,$$ for some coefficients $a_i \in \Z$. For a generic $X$, the families in Section 3 give the following linear relations among the coefficients $$\begin{aligned}16 \cdot a_{1^4} + 4 \cdot a_{1^2 2} + a_{2^2} &= 4320 \text{ from (3.5)}\\ 625 a_{1^{4}} + 125 a_{1^{2}2} - 25a_{13} + 25a_{2^2} - 6 a_{4} &= 103680 \text{ from (3.9)}\\ 3436a_{1^4} + 1076 a_{1^{2}2} +116 a_{13} + 316 a_{2^2} &= 1036800 \text{ from (3.14)}\\ 6a_{1^{4}} + 21a_{1^{2}2}+6a_{13}+16a_{2^{2}}+a_{4} &= 25920 \text{ from (3.26)}\\ a_{2^2} &= 0 \text{ from (3.29)}\\ a_{1^4} - a_{1^2 2}- a_{13} + a_{2^2} &= 0 \text{ from (3.30)}\\ 16a_{1^4} -4a_{1^2 2}- 4a_{13} + a_{2^2} &= 0 \text{ from (3.31)}\\ 81a_{1^4} +9a_{1^2 2}- 9a_{13} + a_{2^2}-2a_4 &= 0 \text{ from (3.32)}.\end{aligned}$$ These equations uniquely determine all the coefficients $a_i$ to be as stated.
