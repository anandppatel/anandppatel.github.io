Proof. (0C55)

There exists an open $W \subset \mathcal M$ such that $m^{-1} (W) \to W$ is a finite étale morphism. Then the degree of $m$ is simply the number of points in $m^{-1}(u)$ for $u \in W$. By shrinking $W$ if necessary, we may assume that the map $$\mathscr M^{ss} \to \mathcal M$$ is an isomorphism over $W$.

Let $U$ be as in the definition a good family. Then, for every $u \in U$, we have the equality $$\cX \times_{\mathscr M} u = \cX \times_{\mathscr M} \overline u.$$ By shrinking $U$ if necessary, we may assume that $U$ consists of semistable points and maps to $W \subset \mathcal M$ in the coarse space. Then, for every $u \in U$, we have $$\begin{aligned}\deg \mu^* \Orb(u) &= \# \left(\cX \times_{\mathscr M} u \right)\\ &= \# \left(\cX \times_{\mathcal M} u\right) \\ &= \deg m.\end{aligned}$$
