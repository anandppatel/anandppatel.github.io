Proof of Proposition 3.6 [B343]. (2477)

Recall that $\mathcal{X} \to \overline{\M}_{0,7}$ is the cubic surface associated to $\mathcal{Z} \subset \mathcal{P}$ in the $\P^2$-bundle $\rho \colon \mathcal{P} \to \overline{\M}_{0,7}$. By construction, we have $$\mathcal{V} = \rho_*(\mathcal{I}_{\mathcal{Z}} \otimes \omega^{-1}_{\rho}).$$ Hence, in the Grothendieck group of $\overline{\M}_{0,7}$, we have $$[\mathcal{V}] = \rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}) - \rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\mathcal{Z}}).$$ By the relative Euler sequence, we see that $\omega^{-1}_{\rho}$ is isomorphic to $\O_{\mathcal{P}}(3) \otimes \rho^* \det (\Sym^2 \mathcal{A})^{\vee}$, and hence the first term in (3.6) is $$\rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}) = \Sym^3(\Sym^2 \mathcal{A}) \otimes \mathcal{L}^3.$$ Since $\mathcal{Z}$ is the disjoint union $\Sigma \cup \tau$, the second term is a sum $$\rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\mathcal{Z}}) = \rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\tau}) + \rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\Sigma}).$$ Since $\tau$ is defined by the surjection $\Sym^2 \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{L}^{-1}$, the restriction $\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\tau}$ is trivial. To compute the restriction to $\Sigma$, we view $\Sigma$ as a divisor in $\overline{\mathcal{C}} = \P A^{\vee}$ and let $D = \omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\overline C} = \O(6) \otimes \overline{\phi}^* \mathcal{L}^3$. Thus, in the Grothendieck group, we have $$\rho_*(\omega_{\rho}^{-1}|_{\Sigma}) = \overline{\phi}_*\left(D\right) - \overline{\phi}_* \left( D(-\Sigma)\right).$$ We now use Lemma 3.7 [38DB] with the section $\sigma_{\infty}$ to compute the terms on the right hand side of (3.8). Putting this together with (3.6) and (3.7), we get $$[\mathcal{V}] = \mathcal{L}^{-3}+ \mathcal{L}^{1}+ \mathcal{L}^{-1}+ \mathcal{L}^{-2}$$ in the Grothendieck group. The result is now a simple computation using $c_1(\mathcal{L}) = \psi_{\infty}$ and $\int_{\overline{\M}_{0,7}} \psi_{\infty}^4 = 1$.
