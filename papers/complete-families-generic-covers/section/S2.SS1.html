2.1. Divisor class computations

Proposition 2.11. (454C)

In $\Pic (\HdgX) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ we have: $$\begin{aligned}[\Bog(\mathcal{E})] &= \epsilon_2 - \frac{d-2}{2d-2} \epsilon_{1,1}\\ [\Bog(\mathcal{F})] &= \varphi_{2} - \frac{1}{2}\varphi_{1,1} + \frac{d-3}{d}\epsilon_{1,1}\\ (5+6/d)\lambda - \delta &= [\Bog(\mathcal{F})] + \frac{(6-d)(d-1)}{d}[\Bog(\mathcal{E})]\end{aligned}$$

Proof. (01FE)

Recall that if $\mathcal{V}$ is a vector bundle of rank $r$ on a stack $Z$ then the Bogomolov expression $\Bog(\mathcal{V})$ is given by $$\Bog(\mathcal{V}) = \frac{1}{2r}c_{1}^{2}\mathcal{V} - \ch_{2}\mathcal{V}.$$ If $\mathcal{V}$ is a vector bundle on a family of curves, the pushforward of the Bogomolov expression to the base of the family gives the divisor class of the locus where the vector bundle restricts to an unstable bundle, assuming the general fiber is stable (or regular poly-stabe in the case of genus $1$ curves.)

And so the proposition follows from this and Proposition 2.8 [9D30].

Corollary 2.12. (B932)

The relation $$(5+6/d) \lambda - \delta = 0$$ holds in the rational Picard group $\Pic(\St) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$.

Proof. (7E17)

Immediate from Proposition 2.11 [454C].

Corollary 2.13. (0F19)

Suppose $B$ is a complete curve and $\beta: B \to \St$ is a morphism. Then $$(5+6/d) \deg \beta^{*} \lambda - \deg \beta^{*} \delta = 0.$$

Proof. (0DCC)

Immediate from Proposition 2.11 [454C].

In light of Corollary 2.13 [0F19], it makes sense to pose the slightly weaker conjecture:

Conjecture 2.14. (09ED)

There are plenty of complete curves in $\Hurfixed{d}{g}(X)$ which have slope $5+6/d$.

Clearly, Conjecture 2.10 [2541] implies Conjecture 2.14 [09ED].

Theorem 2.15. (3E58)

Suppose $D \subset \Mgbar$ is an effective divisor with divisor class $a \lambda - b \delta$. If Conjecture 2.14 [09ED] is true for $d$ sufficiently large, then $$a/b \geq 5.$$

Proof. (6BAA)

Assume Conjecture 2.14 [09ED], and let $d$ be any of the infinitely many integers satisfying Corollary 2.4 [9806]. Let $\beta: B \to \Hurstable{d}{g}(X)$ be a complete curve realizing the truth of Conjecture 2.14 [09ED]. Then, since $B$ passes through a general point and since $\varphi^{-1}(D) \neq \Hurfixed{d}{g}(X)$, it follows that $$\deg \beta^{*}(D) = a \deg \beta^{*} \lambda - b \deg \beta^{*} \delta \geq 0.$$

As $\beta^{*} \deg \lambda \geq 0$ (in fact strictly so) for a non-constant proper family of curves with smooth general member, the theorem now follows from Corollary 2.13 [0F19] by letting $d \to \infty$.
