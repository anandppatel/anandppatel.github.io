Proof. (11ED)

Let $\alpha:X \to \chain{a+b}$ be a general cover in $\Sigma(d;0, ..., 0,1 , ...1)$. Split it into the $a$ rational components $X_R \to \chain{a}$ and the $b$ genus one components $X_E \to \chain{b}$.

The forgetful map $\Sigma(d;0, ..., 0,1 , ...1) \to \Sigma(d;0, ..., 0)$ (which sends the cover $X \to \chain{a+b}$ to $X_R \to \chain{a}$) is dominant. Hence, if we assume Proposition 4.4 [9AD8] is true when $b=0$, we may assume the $F$-bundle of $X_R \to \chain{a}$ is balanced.

Moreover, by Item 4 of Lemma 2.7 [8A3F], the $F$-bundle of $X_E \to \chain{b}$ is not only balanced, it is trivial, up to a twist by a line bundle. Hence, the $F$-bundle of $\alpha: X\to \chain{a+b}$—which is obtained by gluing the (balanced) $F$-bundle of $X_R \to \chain{a}$ with the (trivial, up to twist) $F$-bundle of $X_E \to \chain{b}$—is balanced as well.
