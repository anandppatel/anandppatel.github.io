Proof. (421F)

Let $\nu \from \coprod_{i} P_{i} \to \chain{}$ be the total normalization map. Consider the exact sequence of $\sh{O}_{\chain{}}$-sheaves $$0 \to \End V \to \nu_{*}\nu^{*}(\End V) \to \oplus_{i} \End (k(p_{i})^{r}) \to 0.$$ The last part of the long exact sequence of cohomology looks like $$\ldots \to H^{1}(\chain{}, \End V) \to H^{1}(\chain{}, \nu_{*}\nu^{*}\End V) \to 0.$$ Since $\nu$ is finite, we can identify the vector space $H^{1}(\chain{}, \nu_{*}\nu^{*}\End V)$ with $H^{1}(\coprod P_{i}, \nu^{*}\End V) = \oplus_{i}H^{1}(P_{i}, \End V_{i})$. Therefore, if $H^{1}(\chain{}, \End V) = 0$, then $\oplus_{i}H^{1}(P_{i}, \End V_{i}) = 0$, which proves the lemma.
