Lemma 5.5. (C6FD)

Let $L \subset \P^r$ be a line, and $H\subset \P^r$ a hyperplane transverse to $L$, and $R \subset H$ a rational normal curve. Let $V \subset H^0(\sh{I}_{L \cup R}(2))$ be a non-empty linear series. Assume:

  1. no element of $V$ is a reducible quadric containing $H$,
  2. the general element $Q_\text{gen} \subset \P^r$ of $V$ is such that $L \not\subset \Sing Q_\text{gen}$.

Then for general choices of $p\in L$ and $q \in R$:

  • The line $L_\text{gen} = \overline{pq}$ is not contained in $Q_\text{gen}$.
  • Among the quadrics $Q \in V$ containing $L_\text{gen}$, a general one does not contain $L_\text{gen}$ in its singular locus.
