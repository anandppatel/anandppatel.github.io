Proof. (4DCF)

To say that $f$ is a sweeping family is to say there exists a proper flat family of curves $$g \from \cS \to B$$ and a dominant morphism $m \from \cS \to \M^{1}_{d,g}$ where $g^{-1}(0)$ = $S$ for some point $0 \in B$. We may assume $B$ is integral and normal.

Let $$q \from \Mg \to ø{M}_{g}$$ be the map to the coarse space. Then, by abusing notation, we let $m \from \cS \to ø{M}_{g}$ also denote the composite morphism to the coarse space. We then take the fiber product

$$\xymatrix @C -1.1pc { \cS \times_{ø{M}_g} ø{H}_{d,g}^{(2)} \ar[rr]^{m'} \ar[d]_f&& ø{H}_{d,g}^{(2)} \ar[d]^F \\ \cS \ar[rr]_{m} && ø{M}_{g}}$$

The inequality $d \leq g/2 + 1$ implies that the forgetful map $F$ is generically finite onto the $d$-gonal locus. Since $F$ dominates the $d$-gonal locus, we conclude that $f$ is dominant. Therefore, we may choose a component $Z \subset \cS \times_{ø{M}_g} ø{H}_{d,g}^{2}$ dominating $B$.

Now we appeal to the fact that $\Hdgnt$ is a ``global quotient'' stack (see [EHKV01]), that is to say there exists a finite cover $Z' \to Z$ with a morphism $\widetilde{m}' \from Z' \to ø {\H}_{d,g}^{2}$ lifting the morphism $m'$.

We replace $Z'$ with its normalization and consider the natural surjective map $t \from Z' \to B$. Since $Z'$ is normal, the general fiber of $\widetilde{m}'$ will be a smooth curve $S'$, and will map finitely onto the corresponding fiber of the original family $g \from \cS \to B$. The lemma follows by considering the family of admissible covers parametrized by $S'$, i.e. $s(S') = s(S)$.
