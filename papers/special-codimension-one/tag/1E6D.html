Proof. (1E6D)

Suppose $g_{R}$ is even. Then by repeatedly using corollary 4.4 [03FD], we see: In order to deduce $c(\Delta, \sfY) \geq 0$ it suffices to show $c(\Delta_0, {\sfY) \geq 0$ for the boundary divisor $\Delta_{0}$ which has the dual graph $\Gamma_{\Delta_{0}}$}: $$\xygraph{ !{<0cm,0cm>;<1cm,0cm>:<0cm,1cm>::} !{(0,0) }*{\bullet}="l"!{(-.3,.2)}{\scriptstyle v_L}!{(-.3,-.21)}{\scriptstyle g-2} !{(2,0) }*{\bullet}="r"!{(2.3,.2)}{\scriptstyle v_R}!{(2.3,-.2)}{\scriptstyle 0} "l"-@/^.3cm/"r" "l"-"r" "l"-@/_.3cm/"r" }$$

To do this, we use the same type of pencil $p$ as in Proposition 4.1 [44CA], varying the genus $0$ component. Fortunately, as is the case for rational partial pencils, the split fiber does not contribute any intersection with further boundary components, because the residual curve $R_0$ is a disjoint union of $(1,0)$ curves on $\F = \P^{1} \times \P^{1}$ which are blown down as in section 2.13.1.

Therefore, the resulting rational partial pencil family has the following intersection numbers: $$\begin{aligned}\lambda \cdot p &= 0\\ \delta \cdot p &= 3\\ \Delta_0 \cdot p &= -1\\ {\sfX} \cdot p &\geq 0\end{aligned}$$

By intersecting with $\sfX = a\lambda - b\delta - {\sfY}$ we deduce $c(\Delta_0, {\sfY}) \geq 0$.

The analysis for $g_R$ odd is completely analogous. We simply need to show that $c(\Delta_{1}, {\sfY}) \geq 0$ where $\Delta_{1}$ has an elliptic curve as the right hand component. Again, we just use a partial pencil family on on the surface $\F_{1}$. We leave the details to the reader.
