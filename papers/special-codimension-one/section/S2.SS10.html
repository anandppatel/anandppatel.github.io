2.10. Intersection multiplicities.

We now indicate how to compute intersection multiplicities of a one parameter family lying entirely in the boundary with boundary divisors. For this, we review the description of the deformation spaces of admissible covers found in [HM82].

Pick any boundary divisor $\Delta \subset \Hdg^{\rm{adm}}$, and choose a general admissible cover $[\alpha \colon C \longrightarrow P] \in \Delta$. Let $p \in P$ be the unique node, and let $(r_1,r_2,...,r_k)$ be the local degrees of $\alpha$ occurring at the nodes $(q_1,...,q_k)$ above $p$. A description of the complete local ring $\compl{\cO}_{\Hdg^{\rm{adm}}, \alpha}$ is given by Harris and Mumford in [HM82] as: $$\compl{\cO}_{\Hdg^{\rm{adm}}, \alpha} = \Spec k[[z_{1}, ... , z_{b-4}, t_1,t_2, ... , t_k, s]]/ (t_1^{r_1} = t_2^{r_2} = ... = t_k^{r_k} = s).$$ This presentation is such that $$\compl{\cO}_{[ø{\M}_{0,b}/\mathfrak{S}_{b}], \Br(\alpha)} = k[[z_{1}, ... ,z_{b-4}, s]]$$ and the obvious map $\compl{\cO}_{[ø{\M}_{0,b}/\mathfrak{S}_{b}], \Br(\alpha)} \to \compl{\cO}_{\Hdg^{\rm{adm}}, \alpha}$ gives the complete local description of the branch morphism $$\Br^{\rm{adm}} \from \Hdg^{\rm{adm}} \to [\overline{\M}_{0,b}/\mathfrak{S}_{b}].$$

Above the veresal deformation space $Def\, \alpha := \Spf{\compl{\cO}_{\Hdg^{\rm{adm}}, \alpha}}$, there is a versal family: $$\xymatrix @C -1.5pc { C \subset \mathcal{\cC} \ar[rr]^{\compl{\alpha}} \ar[rd]_\varphi&& {P} \subset \mathcal{\cP} \ar[ld]^\pi \\ &Def\, \alpha}$$ Locally around the node $p \in {P} \subset \cP$, $\cP$ has local equation $uv=s$. At the nodes $q_{i} \in C \subset \cC$, $i = 1,2,...,k$, the total space $\cC$ has local equation $x_i y_i = t_i$. In these local coordinates, the admissible cover $\compl{\alpha}$ is locally given by $u \mapsto x_{i}^{r_{i}}$ and $v \mapsto y_{i}^{r_{i}}$. Furthermore, the equations $s = t_{1} = ... = t_{k} = 0$ cuts out the divisor $\Delta$.

This local description of the boundary allows us to understand intersection multiplicities of partial pencil families with boundary components. So let $$\alpha \from \cC \to \cP \to S$$ be a general family contained in $\Delta$. Furthermore, let $R_{i} = L_{i}$, $i=1,...,k$ be the glued sections mapping to a marked section of nodes $0 \subset \cP$, where, around the section $R_{i} = L_{i}$ the map $\alpha$ has local degree $r_{i}$ over $0$.

The admissibility condition at the nodes tells us that $$\deg (N_{L_{i}/\cC_{L}}\otimes N_{R_{i}/\cC_{R}})^{\otimes r_i} = \deg (N_{L_{j}/\cC_{L}}\otimes N_{R_{j}/\cC_{R}})^{\otimes r_j} = \deg (N_{0/\cP_{L}}\otimes N_{0/\cP_{R}}).$$ Here the $N_{\bullet}$ denote normal bundles.

Suppose furthermore that $S \to \Hdg^{\rm{adm}}$ comes from a family $S \to \Hdgn$, i.e. that $S \to \Hdg^{\rm{adm}}$ is the coarse space of a twisted partial pencil. The family $S \to \Hdgn$ will have intersection number $$S \cdot \Delta' = {\rm deg}\, (N_{0/\cP_{L}}\otimes N_{0/\cP_{R}})/lcm(r_1,...,r_k)$$ with the boundary divisor $\Delta' \subset \Hdg$ in which it lies. (We assume that $S \to \Hdgn$ does not have additional isolated intersections with $\Delta'$, in which case we must add these contributions appropriately.) This follows from the observation that the order of ramification of the branch map from the normalization $$\Br^{\nu} \from (Def\, \alpha)^{\nu} \to \Spf{\compl{\cO}_{[ø{\M}_{0,b}/\mathfrak{S}_{b}], \Br(\alpha)}}$$ is $lcm(r_{1},...,r_{k})$.
