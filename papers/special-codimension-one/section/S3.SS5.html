3.5. The effective divisor class $\sfX$.

Now we notice in Proposition 3.5 [9908] that when $d \leq 5$, the coefficient of $\sfD$ is positive in $\sfM$ and negative in $\sfCE$. We let $X$ be the unique (up to scaling) effective linear combination of $\sfM$ and $\sfCE$ making the coefficient of $\sfD$ zero.

In ${\rm Pic}_{\Q}\widetilde{\H}_{d,g}$ the divisor class $\sfX$ has the form $$\sfX = a\lambda - b\delta$$ for some $a$ and $b$. The reader can check that the ratio $a/b$ is $(13g+15)/2g$ and $(31g + 44)/5g$ when $d = 4$ and $d=5$, respectively. Furthermore, when $d=3$, neither $\sfCE$ nor $\sfD$ exist, and the slope $a/b$ of $\sfM$ is $(7g+6)/g$. These are precisely the numbers $s(\M^{1}_{d,g})$ occurring in slope45.
