Question 5.6. (FB8D)

The first transvectant $(f,d) \mapsto \{f,g\}$ is one of the most important examples of an $\SL(2)$-covariant of binary forms. But there are plenty more covariants, including higher transvectants. Each of these has a fundamental class, by which we mean the fundamental class of the graph closure as in Theorem 4.7. What are these classes?
