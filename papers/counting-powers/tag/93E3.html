Corollary 4.5. (93E3)

For $i = 1,2,3$ let $\zeta_{i}$ denote the divisor class on $\P S_{m} \times \P S_{n} \times \P S_{m+n-2}$ obtained by pulling back the hyperplane class from the $i$-th factor. Suppose $p(\sigma_1,\sigma_2) \in \Z[\sigma_1,\sigma_2]$ is a homogeneous degree $m+n$ polynomial in the two Chern classes $\sigma_1,\sigma_2$ of $\S(d)$ where $\sigma_j$ has degree $j$. Finally, set $$\begin{aligned}\alpha_{1} &= (-a+1)\zeta_1 + (-b+1)\zeta_2 - \zeta_3\\ \alpha_{2} &= -a\zeta_1 \cdot \left(\zeta_1 + (-b+1)\zeta_2 - \zeta_3\right).\end{aligned}$$

Then we get the following equality of integers: $$\int_{\transvectant_{m,n}} p(\sigma_1,\sigma_2) = \int_{\P S_{m} \times \P S_{n} \times \P S_{m+n-2}} p(\alpha_1,\alpha_2) \cap \beta_{*}[\transvectant_{m,n}].$$
