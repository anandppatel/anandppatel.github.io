Section 4. Enumerative geometry of $\transvectant_{m,n}$

To understand $\transvectant_{m,n}$ in any depth, we must introduce coordinates. To that end, we will denote by $$ a = a_{0}x^{m} + {e \choose 1} a_{1}x^{m-1}y + {e \choose 2} a_{2}x^{m-2}y^{2} + \dots + a_{e}y^{e} $$ a general element of $\P S_{e}$. If $$\begin{aligned}f &= f_{0}x^{m} + {m \choose 1} f_{1}x^{m-1}y + {m \choose 2} f_{2}x^{m-2}y^{2} + \dots + f_{m}y^{m}\\ g &= g_{0}y^{n} + {n \choose 1} g_{1}x^{n-1}y + {n \choose 2} g_{2}x^{n-2}y^{2} + \dots + g_{n}x^{n}\end{aligned}$$ are forms of degree $m$ and $n$, respectively, then the transvectant $t = \{f,g\}$ is a form of degree $m+n-2$ whose coefficients are described in the next lemma.

Lemma 4.1. (50FD)

Let $t = t_{0}x^{m+n-2} + t_{1}x^{m+n-3}y + t_{2}x^{m+n-4}y^{2} + \dots + t_{m+n-2}y^{m+n-2}$ be the transvectant of $f$ and $g$.

  1. The coefficient $t_k$ is a homogeneous sum of monomials of the form $f_i g_j$ for $i+j = k+1$. Every monomial $f_i g_j$ satisfying $i+j = k+1$ appears with non-zero coefficient in $t_k$.
  2. If $f_i, g_j, f_j, g_i$ all exist, then the coefficients of $f_i g_j$ and $f_j g_i$ in $t_{i+j-1}$ sum to zero.

Proof. (BFDE)

This follows from direct calculation of the first transvectant.
