Proof. (E983)

The closure of the \(\GL W\)-orbit of \([S']\) is precisely the image of the morphism $$\widetilde \phi \colon \widetilde \P \to \Gr(r+1, \Sym^d W).$$ Let \(\widetilde \Delta \subset \widetilde \P\) be the pre-image of \(\Delta \subset \P^3\). Then \(\widetilde \P - \widetilde \Delta\) is a copy of \(\PGL_3\), and its image is the orbit of \([S']\).

Consider \(\widetilde m \in \widetilde \Delta\) and let \(m \in \Delta\) be its image. Suppose \(m\) does not lie on any line \(L_b\) and let \(\im m = [w_1] \in \P W\). Then by Corollary 4.4 [22D8], the image \(\widetilde \phi(\widetilde m) \in \Gr(r+1, \Sym^d W)\) corresponds to $$\langle w_1^d w_2^{0}, \dots, w_1^{d-r}w_2^{r}\rangle,$$ where \(w_2 \in W\) is any vector linearly independent from \(w_1\).

Suppose \(m\) lies on \(L_b\). Let \(w = \im m \in \P W\), so that \(m \in \Delta\) corresponds to \((b,w)\) in the isomorphism (4.3). Suppose \(v_2 \in V\) is such that \(b = [v_2]\) and \(w_1 \in W\) such that \(w = [w_1]\). The point \(\widetilde m\) corresponds to a direction in the normal bundle \(N_{L_b/\P^3}\) at \(p\). Consider the direction in \(N_{L_b/\P^3}|_p\) corresponding to the line \(\P V \times \{w\} \subset \Delta\) through \(m\). By Corollary 4.4 [22D8], the map \(\widetilde \phi\) is constant on this line with value given by the subspace (4.5). If \(\widetilde m\) corresponds to this direction, then \(\widetilde \phi(\widetilde m)\) corresponds to the same subspace. All the other directions in \(N_{L_b/\P^3}|_p\) can be realised by tangent directions of families \(m_t\) for suitable choices of \(v_1 \in V\) and \(w_2 \in W\). If \(\widetilde m\) corresponds to the tangent direction given by \(m_t\), then Proposition 4.3 [38C5] implies that \(\widetilde \phi(\widetilde m)\) corresponds to the subspace $$\langle w_1^{d-a_0}w_2^{a_0}, \dots, w_1^{d-a_r}w_2^{a_r}\rangle.$$

In conclusion, we see that the image of every point of \(\widetilde \Delta\) is of the form (4.5), which corresponds to the generic vanishing sequence, or (4.6), which corresponds to the vanishing sequence of a ramification point.
