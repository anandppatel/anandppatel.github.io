Proposition 2.4. (9951)

Consider sections \(g_1, \dots, g_n \from \eta \to G_\eta\) such that for all \(i \neq j\), the section \(g_ig_j^{-1}\) does not extend to a section \(\Delta \to G_\Delta\). Suppose \(\overline m_1, \dots, \overline m_n \in M\) are limits of \(g_1 m_\eta, \dots, g_n m_\eta\). Then the cycle \(\Sigma - \WOrb(\overline m_1) - \dots - \WOrb(\overline m_n)\) is effective.
