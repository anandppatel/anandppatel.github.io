Proof. (F006)

Let \(\pi \colon W^* \to \P W\) be the projection. Since the image of \(G\) contains the scalars, we have $$ \Orb(w) = \pi^* \Orb([w]),$$ and hence $$|\Gamma| \cdot \Orb(w) = |\Gamma| \cdot \pi^* \Orb([w]).$$ Since \(\phi\) is a complete parametrisation of the \(\overline G\) orbit of \([w]\), we have $$ |\Gamma| \cdot \Orb([w]) = \phi_*[X].$$ We now compute the right hand side.

On \(X \times \P W\), let \(\pi_i\) for \(i = 1,2\) be the two projections. Consider the composite $$ \pi_2^*O(-1) \to \underline W \to \pi_1^*\phi^*Q.$$ Its vanishing locus is precisely the graph of \(\phi \colon X \to \P W\). Therefore, we have $$\phi_*[X] = {\pi_2}_* c_{\rm top} \Hom(\pi_2^*O(-1), \pi_1^* \phi^*Q).$$

In (2.2), we substitute \(\phi_*[X]\) from (2.3) and use the push-pull formula on the fiber square $$ \text{[Commutative diagram — see PDF]} $$ Let \(\widetilde \pi_i\) for \(i = 1,2\) be the two projections on \(X \times W^*\). Noting that the pull-back of \(O(-1)\) to \(W^*\) is trivial, in \(A^*_G(W^*)\) we have $$\begin{split} |\Gamma|[\Orb(w)] &= {\widetilde {\pi_2}}_* c_{\rm top} \Hom(O, \widetilde \pi_1^* \phi^*Q)\\ &= \int_X c_{\rm top}(\phi^*Q). \end{split}$$ Since \(A^*_G(W^*) = A^*_G(W)\) in co-dimension less than \(\dim W\), equation (2.4) also holds in \(A^*_G(W)\).
