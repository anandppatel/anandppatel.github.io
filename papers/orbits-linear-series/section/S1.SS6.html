1.6. Conventions

Throughout, \(\k\) denotes an algebraically closed field of characteristic 0 or of characteristic \(> d\). All schemes are of finite type over \(\k\). A variety is a reduced scheme, separated over \(\k\). Given a vector space \(V\), the notation \(\underline V\) denotes the trivial vector bundle with fiber \(V\) on the scheme that is clear from the context. Given a vector bundle \(V\), the projectivisation \(\P V\) is the space of one-dimensional sub-bundles. Unless mentioned otherwise, point means a closed point. Given a variety \(X\) with a \(G\)-action, \(A^i_G(X)\) denotes the \(G\)-equivariant Chow group of \(X\) of cycles of co-dimension \(i\), as defined in [7].
