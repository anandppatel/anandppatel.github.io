1.6. Organization

In Section 2, we give basic definitions, culminating in the precise general definition of $\rho$ (Definition 2.4 [BA97]). The subsequence sections are logically independent of each other and can be read in any order after Section 2.

In Section 3, we prove Theorem 1.1 [A0D8](1) (Proposition 3.1 [B072]). We then introduce the notion of non-defectivity, which generalizes the condition of having a divisorial dual. After establishing basic properties of non-defectivity, we prove Theorem 1.1 [A0D8](2) (Theorem 3.12 [D7AA]).

In Section 4, we prove Theorem 1.3 [7F28] (Theorem 4.2 [DAB3]). In the same section, we derive explicit formulas for the ramification divisors for scrolls in Section 4.1, give the examples advertised in Theorem 1.2 [B817] (Section 4.2), and treat the threefold scrolls in Section 4.3.

In Section 5, the main goal is the proof of Theorem 1.4 [DA58] (Theorem 5.16 [09E6]). For this, we lay the groundwork by doing some low degree cases by hand (Section 5.1). We then recall the theory of (linked) limit linear series for vector bundles of higher rank in Section 5.2, and define the projection-ramification map for linked linear series in Section 5.3 and Section 5.4. By a degeneration argument involving linked linear series, we prove Theorem 1.4 [DA58] in Section 5.5.

In Section 6, we turn to the enumerative problem of finding the degree of $\rho$, namely the results in Theorem 1.5 [589F]. We treat the cases of rational normal curves and quadric hypersurfaces quickly in Section 6.1 and Section 6.2. We devote Section 6.3 to the case of the Veronese surface and Section 6.4 to the case of the quartic surface scroll.
