Proof. (D08D)

We prove the contrapositive. The projection map $\pi_{2}: \mathcal{Y} \to \P \on{End}(V)$ is $PGL(V)$-equivariant. If $\mathcal{A}_{1} = \mathcal{A}_{2}$, then as both are the closures of $PGL \cdot s^{\gamma_{i}}(0)$, $i=1,2$, it follows that there is an element $g \in PGL(V)$ such that $g \cdot s^{\gamma_{1}}(0) = s^{\gamma_{2}}(0).$ By equivariance of $\pi_{2}$ we conclude that $g \cdot \gamma_{1}(0) = \gamma_{2}(0)$.
