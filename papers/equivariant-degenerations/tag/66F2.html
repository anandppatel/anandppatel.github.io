Proof. (66F2)

We will argue the contrapositive.

Suppose $\mathcal{A}_{\gamma_{1}} = \mathcal{A}_{\gamma_{2}} = \mathcal{A}$. Then both points $(0,\gamma_{i}(0), \alpha^{\gamma_{i}}(0))$ are in $\mathcal{A}$, and both have full orbits. As $\mathcal{A}$ is irreducible and has a dense orbit, it follows that both of the above points are in the same $PGL(V)$ orbit.

Since $\pi_{2}: \mathcal{Y}_{0} \to \P \on{End}(V)$ is $PGL(V)$-equivariant, it follows that $ \gamma_{1}(0) = \gamma_{2}(0)$ are also in the same $PGL(V)$-orbit in $\P \on{End}(V)$, which is what we needed to show.
