Lemma 5.1. (3B9D)

In the setting of Section 5.1, the curve $\alpha^{\gamma}(0) \in \P \on{Sym}^{d}V^{\vee}$ has equation $X \cdot F(0,Y,Z)+G(0,Y,Z) = 0$.

\begin{comment} Suppose $\{L=0\}$ intersects both $\{G=0\}$ and $\{F=0\}$ at general points. Consider the family of degree $d+m$ curves parameterized by $\mb{A}^1$ given by the equation $$\begin{aligned}tF(X,Y,Z)+L(X,Y,Z)G(X,Y,Z).\end{aligned}$$ Then, there is a 1-parameter family of matrices $\gamma: \mb{A}^1\backslash\{0\}\to PGL_3$ such that the limiting matrix $\lim_{t\to 0}{\gamma(t)}$ in the $\mb{P}^8$ of $3\times 3$ matrices modulo scalars has image precisely the line cut out by $L$. Furthermore, the limit $$\begin{aligned}\lim_{t\to 0}tF(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))+L(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))G(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))\end{aligned}$$ in the $\mb{P}^{\binom{d+2}{2}-1}$ of degree $d$ plane curves a general degree $d$ plane curve with a $d-1$-fold multiplicity point. \end{comment}
