Proof. (41C9)

\begin{comment} Without loss of generality assume $p=[0:0:1]$ and $Q=X^2+YZ$. Let $\gamma(t)$ act diagonally by sending $X\to tX$, $Y\to t^2Y$, $Z\to Z$. The limit $\lim_{t\to 0}{\gamma(t)}$ has image $[0:0:1]$ as claimed. Now, to evaluate $$\begin{aligned}\lim_{t\to 0}{t^3F(t^2X,tY,Z)+Q(t^2X,tY,Z)^2},\end{aligned}$$ \end{comment} Note $Q(tX,t^{2}Y,Z)^2=t^4Q(X,Y,Z)$. Also, the only coefficients of $t^3F(tX,t^{2}Y,Z)$ whose vanishing order with respect to $t$ is at most 4 are the coefficients of $Z^4$ and $Z^3 X$. Since $F$ vanishes at $p=[0:0:1]$ by assumption, the coefficient of $Z^4$ is zero.

The tangent line to $\{Q=0\}$ at $p$ is given by $Y=0$. Since $\{F=0\}$ is transverse to $\{Q=0\}$ at $p$, the coefficient of $Z^3X$ is nonzero. Therefore, $$\begin{aligned}\lim_{t\to 0}t^{-4}(t^3F(t^2X,tY,Z)+Q(t^2X,tY,Z)^2)=(X^2+YZ)^2+aZ^3X\end{aligned}$$ for some constant $a\neq 0 \in \k$, which is the unique, up to projective equivalence, rational curve with an $A_6$ singularity having a full dimensional orbit, as found in [AF00b, Example 5.4]. This is the curve $C_{A_{6}}$
