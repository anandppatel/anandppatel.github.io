Proof. (6077)

\begin{comment} Clearly the coefficient of each monomial of $G(tX,Y,Z)$ is divisible by $t$ if and only if the monomial is divisible by $X$, and similarly each monomial of $XF(tX,Y,Z)$ is divisible by $t$ if and only if the monomial is divisible by $X^2$. \end{comment} Unraveling the definition of the family of curves $\alpha^{\gamma}: U \to \P \on{Sym}^{d}V^{\vee}$, it suffices to prove $$\begin{aligned}\lim_{t\to 0}t^{-1}((tX)F(tX,Y,Z)+tG(tX,Y,Z))&=X \cdot F(0,Y,Z)+G(0,Y,Z),\end{aligned}$$ which is immediate. \begin{comment} Since $\{F=0\}$ or $\{G=0\}$ does not contain $\{X=0\}$, there is at least one such monomial not divisible by $X$ and the limit is achieved by setting $X$ to be zero in $F(X,Y,Z)$ and $G(X,Y,Z)$. \end{comment} \begin{comment} Without loss of generality, we can assume $L(X,Y,Z)=X$. Let $\gamma(t)$ be the diagonal matrix sending $X\to tX$, $Y\to Y$, $Z\to Z$. Then, $$\begin{aligned}\lim_{t\to 0}tF(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))+L(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))G(\gamma(t)\cdot (X,Y,Z))\end{aligned}$$ is $F(0,Y,Z)+XG(0,Y,Z)$. Since $\{F=0\}$ and $\{G=0\}$ intersect $\{L=0\}$ at general points, $F(0,Y,Z)+XG(0,Y,Z)$ cuts out a general curve with a point of multiplicity of $d-1$ at $[1:0:0]$. \end{comment}
