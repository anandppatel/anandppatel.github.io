Proof. (2B2E)

We consider a family of smooth quartic curves, where the general member $C'$ has no hyperflexes, and with $C$ as the special fiber.

Applying Lemma 7.13 [DE72], once per hyperflex, (also see Remark 7.15 [FE4C]) and using principle (Remark 7.14 [765E] shows that the hypotheses are met), we see that it suffices to check that the predegree of $C'$ is the predegree of $C$ plus $n$ times the predegree of $C_{E_6}$. The predegree of $C$ is $294n$ less than the predegree of $C'$ [AF93b, Section 3.6]. Also, the predegree of $C_{E_6}$ is 294 from [AF00b, bottom of page 36] or Corollary 4.4 [98EB] (noting $\#\on{Aut}(C_{E_6})=2$).

Finally, we use $\p_{C'}=8\p_{C_{A_6}}$ from Theorem 7.7 [1985].
