Proof of jdegeneration. (8B2A)

Let $W$ denote the smooth variety parametrizing triples $(C,L,p)$ where $C$ is a plane cubic, $p \in C$ is a point and $L$ is a line containing $p$ which meets $C$ with multiplicity at least $3$ at $p$. ($W$ is similar to the variety $V$ from the proof of Lemma 6.8 [13BA], however we allow $C$ to be an arbitrary smooth cubic curve. Therefore, $V$ is a closed subvariety of $W$.)

The $9$ dimensional variety $W$ has a natural projection to the projective space $\mb{P}^{9}$ of cubic plane curves, and also has a projection to the projective space $\mb{P}^{2*}$ of lines in $\mb{P}^{2}$. We let $H$ and $h$ denote the divisor classes on $W$ corresponding to the respective pullbacks of $\mc{O}(1)$ under these projections. Just as in the proof of Lemma 6.8 [13BA], the divisor class $H + (d-3)h$ is the pullback of $\mc{O}(1)$ under the map $$f: W \to \mb{P}^{{d+2 \choose 2}-1}$$ sending $(C,L,p)$ to the curve $C + (d-3)L$.

For each $j\in \mb{P}^1$, define the $8$ dimensional subvariety $W_{j} \subset W$ to be the closure of the locus of triples $(C,L,p)$ where $C$ is a cubic with $j$-invariant $j$. Let $W_{\infty,AN},W_{\infty,BN}\subset W_\infty$ be the two components of $W_\infty$, where $W_{\infty,AN}$ consists of triples $(C,L,p)$ where $C$ is singular at $p$ and $W_{\infty,BN}$ is the closure of the triples $(C,L,p)$ where $C$ is smooth at $p$. By specializing $j$ to $\infty$, we get $$\begin{aligned}[W_{j}]\simeq A[W_{\infty,AN}]+B[W_{\infty,BN}]+C[Z],\end{aligned}$$ with $A,B,C$ positive integers and $j$ general. Here, $Z$ consists of $(C,L,p)$ where $C$ is the union of a conic and $L$ and $p$ is on $L$. Now, we intersect both sides of the equation with $H^8$, where $H$ is the hyperplane class pulled back from the $\mb{P}^9$ of cubic plane curves.

Specifically, $[W_{j}]H^8=12\cdot 9=108$, where $12$ is the degree of the orbits closure of a cubic with a fixed $j$-invariant and $9$ is the number of flexes on such a cubic. Similarly, we can compute $[W_{\infty,AN}]H^8=3\cdot 12=36$ and $[W_{\infty,BN}]H^8=2\cdot 12=24$, where we have $3$ smooth flexes of a nodal cubic and $2$ branches at a node, respectively. We know that the intersections are all multiplicity $1$ by Bertini. Finally, $H^8\cdot [Z]=0$.

We conclude by noting that $A=1$ and $B=3$ are the only positive integer solutions to $108=36A+24B$.
