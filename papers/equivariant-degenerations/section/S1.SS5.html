1.5. Acknowledgements

We would like to thank Paolo Aluffi and Joe Harris for helpful conversations. We would also like to thank Carl Lian for a careful reading and helpful comments. Finally, we thank an anonymous referee for helpful suggestions.

\begin{comment} We begin the paper with the case $r=1$, corresponding to orbits of point configurations on $\mb{P}^1$ and first studied by Aluffi and Faber [AF93a]. We give two independent approaches. We show how to extend Aluffi and Faber's computation equivariant setting in Section 8 using the Atiyah-Bott formula. We also show how the same formula can be deduced from the authors' previous work [LPST20]. Finally, in the case where the multiplicities are all 1, we give a simple third argument via slow projection. \end{comment}
