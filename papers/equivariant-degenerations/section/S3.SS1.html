3.1. Orbits of quartic plane curves from degeneration

Our strategy for computing equivariant classes of $PGL(V)$-orbit closures of smooth quartic planes will be to specialize them into 8-dimensional orbits of singular curves, which can then be computed by other means. Our main result is:

Theorem 3.1. (6F86)

Let $C$ be a smooth quartic curve which possesses $m$ hyperflexes. Let $C_{A_n}, C_{E_6}, C_{D_4}$ denote general smooth quartics possessing an $A_n$ singularity, an $E_6$ singularity, and an ordinary triple point respectively. For $0\leq i\leq 3$, let $C_i$ be a quartic curve given by the union of $i$ general lines and a general degree $4-i$ curve. Then \begin{alignat*}{2} P_{C} &= 8P_{C_{A_6}}-mP_{C_{E_6}}\\ P_{C_{A_n}}&= (7-n)P_{C_{A_6}}&\text{for $3\leq n\leq 6$}\\ P_{C_{D_4}}&=\frac{1}{4}(8P_{C_{A_6}}-P_{C_3})\\ P_{C_{i}}-P_{C_{i+1}} &= P_{C_{D_4}} &\text{for $0\leq i\leq 3$}. \end{alignat*}

The polynomials $P_{C_{A_6}}$, $P_{C_{E_6}}$ and $P_{C_3}$ can be determined from Corollary 4.4 [98EB], and Proposition 4.5 [87CC]. This then allows us to determine the explicit expressions for all remaining curves appearing in Theorem 3.1 [6F86]. \end{comment}
