Ramification divisors of general projections

Anand Patel, with A. Deopurkar and E. Duryev
Documenta Mathematica 25 (2020): 1917–1952

Commentary

This paper, joint with A. Deopurkar and E. Duryev, proves a result in classical projective geometry. The motivating question is purposefully vague: In projective geometry, we have some standard geometric constructions. For instance, we often take intersections with a hyperplane. Or we project a variety to another projective space. In the process, we get several auxiliary geometric figures on the original variety — do these figures "vary maximally" with the parameters underlying the construction? For instance, a general projection of a variety to a projective space of the same dimension produces a ramification divisor. Does the divisor vary maximally with the source of projection?

We thoroughly investigate the effect on ramification divisors of perturbations of projections, and in the process we discover some rather peculiar counter-examples to the naive expectation.
