Remark 6.2. (9B45)

The simplest way to produce families $B$ which satisfy condition $(1)$ in Corollary 6.1 [0931] is to take a family of ample complete-intersection curves on $X$. However, such families will typically fail to yield a dominant map $B \to \mathcal{M}_{g,n}$ once the genus $g$ is large, since the moduli spaces $\mathcal{M}_{g,n}$ usually are not unirational. Therefore, the strategy is particularly promising when $g=0$.
