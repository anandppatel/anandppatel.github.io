Proof of Proposition 3.6 [C127]. (1841)

We will prove that $\nabla(D)^p w_{\lambda} \neq \nabla(D^p) w_{\lambda}$, from which the result obviously follows. We now introduce some notation. For any vector $v$, denote by $v[m]$ its $(m+1)^{th}$ entry. We have $$\nabla(D)^p w_{\lambda} = \sum_{W \in \it{I}} W w_{\lambda},$$ where $\it{I}$ is the set of all length $p$ words in the letters $A$ and $D$. Here, $A$ acts on any vector by left-multiplication, and $D$ acts on each coordinate in the natural way.

As in [20], we will show using Lemma 3.8 [082A] that the word $W_0 = AA\hdots A$ ($p$ times) has the property that $|(W_0w_{\lambda})[r-1]|$ is strictly larger than $|(Ww_{\lambda})[r-1]|$ for every $W_0 \neq W \in \it{I}$.

Lemma 3.8 [082A](3) implies that $|w_{\lambda}[m]| \leq \ell^{r-m-1}|w_{\lambda}[r-1]|$. Let $w^j$ and $w_0^j$ be the vectors obtained by applying the first $j$ letters of $W$ and $W_0$ respectively on $w_{\lambda}$. Lemma 3.8 [082A] implies $|w^j[m]| \le \ell^{r-m-1}|w_0^j[m]|$ for any $0\le m \le r-1$. However, $W$ differing from $W_0$, must contain the letter $D$. Suppose that such a letter first occurred at the $j_0^{th}$ stage. Then, Lemma 3.8 [082A] (1) implies that $|w^{j_0}[m]| < \ell^{r-m-1}|w_0^{j_0}[r-1]|$. According to Lemma 3.8 [082A] (2), $|w^{j}[m]| < \ell^{r-m-1}|w_0^{j}[r-1]|$, for $j \ge j_0$. Therefore, $|(W_0w_{\lambda})[r-1]| > |(Ww_{\lambda})[r-1]|$ for every $W \neq W_0$, and hence $|\nabla(D)^p(w_{\lambda})[r-1]|= |(A^p w_{\lambda}) [r-1]| = \ell^p|w_{\lambda}[r-1]|$. On the other hand, by Lemma 3.8 [082A] (1) and the hypothesis that $D^p = D$, $$|\nabla(D^p)(w_\lambda)[r-1]| = |(D+A)(w_\lambda)[r-1]| = \ell|w_{\lambda}[r-1]| .$$ Thus $\nabla(D)^p w_{\lambda} \neq \nabla(D^p) w_{\lambda}$, which is what we needed to show.
