2.3. Returning to the original objective

The stated objective in this paper is to determine $\#\pi^{-1}(\{(q_{1}, \dots, q_{13})\})$ for general points $q_{i} \in \P^{9}$. Using Theorem 2.3, we instead will try to compute $\#\eta^{-1}(\{p_{1}, \dots, p_{13}\})$ for general points $p_{i} \in \P^{2}$. We will still face several obstacles.

The first obstacle arises when we want to refer to the rank $9$ ``vector bundle'' $E$ on $\Hilb_{3}\P^{2}$ whose fiber over a point $[T]$ is the vector space $H^{0}\left(\P^{2}, \left(\O_{\P^{2}}/ \I_{T}^{2}\right)\otimes \O_{\P^{2}}(5)\right).$ With such a bundle we can apply the Porteous formula to access the locus where the natural morphism $\underline{H^{0}\left(\P^{2},\I_{\{p_{1}, \dots, p_{13}\}}(5)\right)} \to E$ has at least a $2$-dimensional kernel. Unfortunately, $E$ is not actually a vector bundle because its rank jumps from $9$ to $10$ over the locus parametrizing fat schemes. And so our attention turns to replacing $\Hilb_{3}\P^{2}$ with a better parameter space, the space of complete triangles $\CT$, which resolves this jumping wrinkle.
