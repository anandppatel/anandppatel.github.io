3.4. Resolving $n$-th power maps

For a non-fat length $3$ scheme $T \subset \P^{2}$ with unique dual $T^{*}$, the squared ideal $\s(T,T^{*}) := \I_{T}^{2}$ is the ideal generated by all pairwise products of elements in $\I_{T}$. This is a length $9$ subscheme when $T$ is not fat. When $T$ is fat, $\I_{T}^{2}$ has length $10$. The key observation is that $\s$ extends to a flat family over all of $\CT$:

Theorem 3.16. (2525)

There exists a flat family $\mathcal{Z} \subset \P^{2} \times \CT$ of length $9$ subschemes parametrized by $\CT$, such that for any $(T,T^{*}) \in \CT$ with $T$ non-fat, the fiber over $(T,T^{*})$ is $\I_{T}^{2}$. More precisely, the fiber over $(T,T^{*})$ is the subscheme defined by the modified ideal $$ \s(T,T^{*}) = \I_{T}^{2} + \I_{T} \cdot \I_{T^{*},1} $$ where $\I_{T^{*},1}$ is the linear part of the ideal of $T^*$ (viewed via duality).

Proof.

The proof proceeds by checking flatness at each orbit type. For the non-fat orbits, $\s(T,T^{*}) = \I_{T}^{2}$ has length $9$. For a fat point $T$ with dual thin scheme $T^{*}$, the modification $\I_{T}^{2} + \I_{T} \cdot \I_{T^{*},1}$ cuts the length down from $10$ to $9$, restoring flatness.

Corollary 3.17. (4A1A)

Let $S$ be a smooth projective surface and let $n \geq 2$. There exists a flat family of length-$\binom{n+1}{2}$ subschemes over the $\CT$-modification of $\Hilb_3 S$, resolving the $n$-th power map.

Proof.

The construction of $\CT$ and the resolution of the squaring map are local in the étale topology, and hence extend to any smooth surface.
