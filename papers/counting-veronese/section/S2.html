Section 2. The correspondence

The fundamental trick for computing $\nu_{3,2}$ is to switch to the task of counting corresponding triples of non-collinear points $\{a,b,c\} \subset \P^2$ satisfying certain conditions relative to 13 prescribed, general points $\Gamma_{13} \subset \P^2$. It is the content of Theorem 2.3 in this section. Whether similar useful correspondences are available for determining other $\nu_{d,n}$'s remains an intriguing question.

The correspondence critically uses Coble's theory of association, and we begin by reviewing this theory following the incisive account in [EP00].
