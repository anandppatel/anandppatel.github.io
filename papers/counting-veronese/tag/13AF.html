Proof. (13AF)

The claim being made is that every length $9$ scheme of the form $\s(T,T^{*})$ imposes independent conditions on degree $n \geq 5$ homogeneous forms. This can either be checked on a case-by-case basis, or can be checked for example on the special complete triangle $(T,T^{*})$ in Proposition 3.15 [AD31], as well as its dual. Then an appeal to semi-continuity gives the conclusion for any complete triangle $(T,T^{*})$ where $T$ is not contained in a line. For those complete triangles $(T,T^{*})$ with $T$ contained in a line, the claim can be checked directly.
