Definition 3.1. (021F)

  1. Let $T \in \Hilb_{3}\P^{2}$ be a length three scheme. $T$'s net of conics is the vector space $\Lambda_{T} := H^{0}(\I_{T}(2)) \subset H^{0}(\O_{\P^{2}}(2)).$
  2. The Jacobian matrix of three homogeneous quadratic forms $Q_{1},Q_{2},Q_{3}$ in the variables $X,Y,Z$ is: $$\begin{aligned}\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial Q_{1}}{\partial X} & \frac{\partial Q_{1}}{\partial Y} & \frac{\partial Q_{1}}{\partial Z} \\ \frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial X} & \frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial Y} & \frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial Z} \\ \frac{\partial Q_{3}}{\partial X} & \frac{\partial Q_{3}}{\partial Y} & \frac{\partial Q_{3}}{\partial Z} \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{aligned}$$
  3. The Jacobian space of $V \subset H^{0}(\P^{2}, \O(2))$ is the vector space spanned by all $2 \times 2$ minors of the Jacobian matrix of any basis $\langle Q_{1}, Q_{2}, Q_{3} \rangle$ of $V$.
  4. If $V \subset H^{0}(\P^{2}, \O_{\P^{2}}(2))$ is $3$-dimensional, we let $V^{*} \subset H^{0}(\check{\P}^{2}, \O_{\check{\P}^{2}}(2))$ denote its apolar $3$-dimensional space.
  5. A scheme $T \subset \P^{2}$ is fat if it is isomorphic to $\spec k[x,y]/(x^{2},xy,y^{2})$. A scheme $T \subset \P^{2}$ is thin if it is contained in a line. We define $\Fat, \Thin \subset \Hilb_{3}\P^{2}$ to be the closed loci of fat and thin schemes, respectively.
  6. A subscheme $T \subset \P^{2}$ (or $\check{\P}^{2}$) consisting of three distinct non-collinear points is called an honest triangle
