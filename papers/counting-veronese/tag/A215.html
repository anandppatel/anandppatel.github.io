Remark 3.2. (A215)

  1. $\Lambda_{T}$ is a $3$-dimensional vector space, no matter the scheme $T$, as is easily checked for each of the $7$ $\PGL(3)$ orbits separately.
  2. Apolarity is the natural pairing between $H^{0}(\P^{2}, \O_{\P^{2}}(2))$ and $H^{0}(\check{\P}^{2}, \O_{\check{\P}^{2}}(2))$, where the latter space is viewed as homogeneous second order differential operators on the former space. So, for instance, the pairing outputs $2$ for the pair $X^{2}, \check{X}^{2}$.
