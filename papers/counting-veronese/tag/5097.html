Proof. (5097)

This follows from combining a dimension count with the condition that $\Gamma_{13}$ is a general set. We only prove the $\Inc$ statement – the other case proceeds mutatis mutandis.

Without losing generality, we need only prove the statement $(T,T^{*},\Lambda) \notin \Inc(p_{1})$. As $\Dom(p_1)$ is irreducible and $24$-dimensional, $$\dim \Dom(p_1) \cap \Inc(p_1) \leq 23.$$ And so, the locally closed set $U := \Dom(p_1) \cap \Inc(p_1) \cap \Fin$ is at most $23$-dimensional.

Every $(T,T^{*},\Lambda) \in U$ determines the $0$-dimensional, length $25$ scheme $\Base(\Lambda) \subset \P^{2}$. Let $$U' \subset U \times (\P^{2})^{12}$$ denote the set parametrizing quadruples $(T,T^{*},\Lambda,W)$ where $(T,T^{*},\Lambda) \in U$ and where $W = (w_1, \dots, w_{12})$ is a $12$-tuple of points in $\P^{2}$ satisfying $w_i \in \Base(\Lambda)$ for all $i$. Then the forgetful map $U' \to U$ is quasi-finite, and hence $$\dim U' \leq 23.$$

The map $U' \to (\P^{2})^{12}$ defined by $(T,T^{*}, \Lambda, W) \mapsto W$ therefore cannot dominate $(\P^{2})^{12}$, and hence the set of $13$ \text{general} points $\{p_1, \dots, p_{13}\}$ cannot be contained in $\Base(\Lambda)$ for $(T,T^{*},\Lambda) \in \bigcap_{i=1}^{13}\Dom(p_{i}) \cap \Inc(p_{1}) \cap \Fin$, implying the lemma.
