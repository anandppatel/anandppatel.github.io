2.3. Proof

Proof of Theorem 1.1. (5C26)
It suffices to prove that the rank $r$ of the sub-bundle $\mathscr{V}\subset\Omega_{g}$ satisfies the congruence $r\equiv 0\pmod{g}$. Again we let $C$ be a curve with two commuting involutions $\tau_{1},\tau_{2}$ with disjoint fixed points of sizes $2m_{1},2m_{2}$ respectively and with quotient $C/\langle\tau_{1},\tau_{2}\rangle\simeq\mathbb{P}^{1}$. Since $y\equiv 0$ by Lemma 2.8, we have $f(m)=-r$ for all $m\in S$. In particular, this means that $\tau_{i}^{*}$ acts as $-\operatorname{id}$ on the fibers of $\mathscr{V}$ for every $i$. Since $\tau_{1}^{*}$ and $\tau_{2}^{*}$ both act as $-\operatorname{id}$ on the rank $r$ sub-bundle, $\tau_{3}^{*}=(\tau_{1}\tau_{2})^{*}$ acts as $\operatorname{id}$ on $\mathscr{V}$. But $\tau_3$ is also an involution, and for a hyperelliptic involution (when $m_3 = g+1$), the eigenvalues on $V_C$ are all $-1$. This forces $r = 0$ unless all eigenvalues on $\mathscr{V}$ are simultaneously $+1$ and $-1$, i.e. $r \equiv 0 \pmod{g}$. Combined with $0 < r \leq g$, we get $r = g$, meaning the sub-bundle is trivial. $\square$
