1.3. Conventions

We will work over the complex numbers $\C$. (Though after making adjustments as in Commentary 2.2 one should be able to prove the result in all characteristics outside $2$ almost immediately from the strategy given. Theorem 1.1 should be true over any field.) The term curve assumes irreducible, smooth, and projective by default, and if $C$ is a curve then $\omega_{C}$ denotes its canonical line bundle. If $G$ is a finite group acting on a curve $C$ then $C/G$ denotes the classical quotient curve rather than the quotient stack.
