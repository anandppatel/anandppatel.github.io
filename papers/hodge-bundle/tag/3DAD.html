Proof. (3DAD)
By the topological classification of surfaces, two orientation-preserving involutions on a closed orientable surface $\Sigma_{g}$ with the same number of fixed points correspond to double covers over a quotient surface of a uniquely determined genus. Thus, such involutions are conjugate in the mapping class group $\operatorname{Mod}_{g}$. The putative bundle $\mathscr{V}$ corresponds to a $\operatorname{Mod}_{g}$-equivariant bundle over the Teichm\"uller space $\mathscr{T}_{g}$, which we continue to denote by $\mathscr{V}$. By the Nielson Realization Theorem [KER83] the fixed point locus $\mathscr{T}_{g}^{\tau}$ of $\tau$ is non-empty and connected. Since $\tau^{*}$ preserves $\mathscr{V}$, and $\tau^{2}=\operatorname{id}$, the eigenvalues of $\tau^{*}$ on the fibers of $\mathscr{V}$ restricted to $\mathscr{T}_{g}^{\tau}$ are $\pm 1$, and by continuity, their multiplicities are constant. Therefore, the trace of $\tau^{*}$ on $\mathscr{V}$ (and hence $V_{C}$) depends only on the conjugacy class of $\tau$ in $\operatorname{Mod}_{g}$, which depends only on $m$. $\square$
