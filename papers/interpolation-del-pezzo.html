Interpolation Problems: Del Pezzo Surfaces

Anand Patel, with A. Landesman
arXiv · Annali Scuola Normale Superiore - Classe di Scienze (2019): 1389–1428

Commentary

Inspired by A. Landesman's undergraduate thesis, we prove that del Pezzo surfaces satisfy interpolation with respect to incidence conditions with linear spaces. The case of 3-Veronese surfaces merits mentioning, because it involves an amusing use of A. Coble's theory of association, now called Gale duality. In particular, we show that through thirteen general points in P^9, there exist a finite, positive, number of 3-Veronese surfaces.

Quite generally, every Veronese variety (i.e. embeddings of projective spaces by complete linear series) comes equipped with a similar enumerative problem, asking how many interpolate through the appropriate number of points. The case of Veronese curves, i.e. rational normal curves is ultra-classical: through n+3 general points in P^n there exists a unique rational normal curve.
