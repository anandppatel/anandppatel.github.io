Proof. (4D72)

Since $B$ is smooth, it is étale locally isomorphic to $\A^{m}.$ So we may reduce to the case $\left( B,b \right) = \left( \A^{m},0 \right)$.

After passing to an open set, the set $Z$ is defined by a single equation $$s^{n} + u_{n-1}s^{n-1}t + \dots + u_{0}t^{n}=0$$ where $u_{i} \in \fm_{0} \subset \Gamma (O_{\A^{m}})$. (Here $\fm_{0}$ is the maximal ideal of the origin.) We may ``complete the $n$-th power'' and find an isomorphic $Z' \subset \P^{1} \times \A^{m}$ defined by a similar equation, but with vanishing $u_{n-1}$ term.

The $n-1$ functions $u_{i} \in \fm_{0} \subset \Gamma \left( O_{\A^{m}} \right), i = 0, \dots n-2$ define a map $\left( \A^{m}, 0 \right) \to \left( \A^{n-1},0 \right)$ with the desired property.
