Proof. (1149)

We recall the stable reduction procedure, from which the proposition is immediately verified.

  1. We view $C := \bigcup \sigma_{i} \subset \P^{1} \times B$ as a curve on a surface, with singularities $q_{i} \in \P^{1}_{b}$.
  2. We resolve the singularities $q_{i} \in C$ by blowing up repeatedly until all branches of $C$ through $q_{i}$ are separated.
  3. Since each blow up in the resolution process happens at smooth centers, the new fiber $P$ of the blown up surface over the point $b \in B$ is the union of the original $\P^{1}_{b}$ and several rational tails attached to $\P^{1}_{b}$ at the points $q_{i}$.
  4. We contract any components of $P$ which have $\leq 2$ special points.

The proposition follows from this description of the stable reduction process.
