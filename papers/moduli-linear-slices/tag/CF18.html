Proof. (CF18)

It suffices to show that the variety $$I_{k}(m_{1}, \dots, m_{k}) \times_{\P^{N}} I_{k}\left( m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \right) \setminus \Delta$$ is irreducible. (Here $\Delta$ is the diagonal.) By Lemma 2.12 [408C] we know that $I_{k,k}''\left( m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \mid m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \right)$ is irreducible. Since this correspondence dominates $I_{k}(m_{1}, \dots, m_{k}) \times_{\P^{N}} I_{k}\left( m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \right) \setminus \Delta$, we conclude the claimed irreducibility, and hence the corollary.
