Proof. (8535)

Let $(X, \ell) \in I_{k-1}(m_{1}+m_{2}, \dots, m_{k})$ be a general point.

All $k$-incident lines of multiplicity $(m_{1}, \dots, m_{k})$ are simple, i.e. occur with multiplicity one, according to Corollary 3.8 [F116]. The line $[\ell]$ occurs as a multiplicity $2$ $k$-incident line of type $(m_{1}, \dots, m_{k})$, according to Corollary 3.5 [2780].

Therefore, above the point $[X] \in \P^{N}$, the correspondence $I_{k}(m_{1}, \dots, m_{k})$ has exactly one nonreduced point of length $2$, and all other points are reduced.

Since the curve $Z_{m_{1},m_{2}} \subset \A^{m_{1}+m_{2}-1}$ is irreducible, the two nearby $k$-incident lines (for a nearby hypersurface $X'$) limiting to $[\ell]$ experience monodromy.

Hence we obtain a simple transposition in the monodromy group $G$.
