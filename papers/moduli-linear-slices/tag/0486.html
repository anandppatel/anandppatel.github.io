Proof. (0486)

This is a consequence of Corollary 2.26 [BC02]. Indeed, étale locally around every $[\ell] \in \G\left( 1,r \right)$, we may assume $\pi : \Sigma \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ is isomorphic to products of maps of the form $$\begin{aligned}\left\{ (z,a_{0}, \dots ,a_{n-2}) \mid z^{n} + a_{n-2}z^{n-2} + \dots a_{0}=0 \right\} \to (a_{0}, \dots ,a_{n-2})\end{aligned}$$ If $r_{1}, \dots r_{n}$ denote the roots of $z^{n} + a_{n-2}z^{n-2} + \dots + a_{0}$, then the variety $$\Spec k\left[ r_{1}, \dots r_{n}\right]/(r_{1}+\dots+ r_{n}=0)$$ maps to $\Spec k\left[ a_{n-2}, \dots , a_{0}, \right]$ by the elementary symmetric expressions in the $r_{i}$.

This, in turn, means that the scheme $\Sigma^{\left[ d \right]}$, above the point $\left[ \ell \right] \in \G\left( 1,r \right)$, is étale locally isomorphic to a product of affine spaces $\Spec k[r_{1}, \dots, r_{n}]/(r_{1} + \dots + r_{n})$, and in particular smooth.
