Proof of Theorem 3.1 [6C21]. (E509)

We apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] to our situation, where $\cM$ is an open subset of $\operatorname{Mor}(\mathbb{P}^1,Z)$ containing $[f]$ and $\cU=\PP^1\times \cM$. Then, applying Lemma 3.9 [E1C1] shows $T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}$ is the direct sum of the Lazersfeld Mukai bundle of $f^{*}T_Z$ with $T_{\PP^1}$. By Lemma 3.10 [FBF7], the Lazersfeld Mukai bundle of $f^{*}T_Z$ is a sum of $\OO(-1)$'s, so $T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}$ is an extension of $\OO(2)$ by a direct sum of $\OO(-1)$'s implying $\mu^{\min}(T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}) \geq -1$.

Suppose $f^{*}E$ splits as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1 \geq \cdots \geq a_r$ and $a_j \leq a_{j+1}-2$. Letting $S=\oplus _{i\leq j}\cO(a_i)$, we find $$\begin{aligned}a_j + (-1) &>a_{j+1}\\ \mu^{\min}(S)+\mu^{\min}(T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}})&>\mu^{\max}((f^{*}E)/S),\end{aligned}$$ Therefore, we can apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] and conclude. \begin{comment} Let $\cM$ be a smooth open subset of the Hilbert scheme of curves in $Z$ containing the class of $C$, and let $\cU \to \cM$ denote the universal family. Then the natural map $\pi: \cU \to X$ has connected fibers by hypothesis. We can pull back $E$ to $\cU$ and the restriction to the fibers of $\cU \to \cM$ will be the same as the restriction of $E$ to the corresponding curves.

Suppose $E|_C$ splits as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1 \geq \cdots \geq a_r$ and $a_j \leq a_{j+1}-2$. Letting $S=\oplus _{i\leq j}\cO(a_i)$, we find $$\begin{aligned}a_j + (-1) &>a_{j+1}\\ \mu^{\min}(S)+\mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/\cU_p}|_C)&>\mu^{\max}(E|_{C}/S),\end{aligned}$$ as $T_{\cU/\cU_p}|_C$ is a direct sum of $\cO(-1)$ by Lemma 3.10 [FBF7]. Therefore, we can apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] and conclude. \end{comment}
