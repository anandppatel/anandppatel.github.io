Proposition 4.14. (B98F)

Assume $(P,s_{1}, \dots , s_{d}) \in \barm_{0,d}$ is a stable curve which is the nodal union of:

  1. a $\P^{1}$ with $n$ marked points $p_{1}, \dots, p_{n}$, and
  2. $k-n$ chains $T_{i}$, $i = 1, \dots k-n$, with each $T_{i}$ intersecting $\P^{1}$ once at a point $q_{i}$, every component of $T_{i}$ having exactly three special points, and each $T_{i}$ containing $a_{i}>1$ marked points.
Suppose $(P, s_{1}, \dots, s_{d})$ is the stable replacement for $D \subset \P^{1}_{b}$ in Section 4.3.

Then

  1. $D \subset \P^{1}_{b}$ is supported on $\leq k$ points.
  2. If $D$ is supported on $k$ points, and if we assume $k \geq 4$, then $D$ must equal $p_{1}+ \dots + p_{n} + a_{1}q_{1} + \dots a_{k-n}q_{k-n}$.
