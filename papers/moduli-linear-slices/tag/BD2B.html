Remark 5.1. (BD2B)

There is one instance in the literature worth noting. In [D'S88], it is argued that the monodromy group of the set of $4$-incident lines of type $(2,2,2,2)$ to an octic surface $X \subset \P^{3}$ is the full symmetric group. Unfortunately, it appears (to the author) that there is a small gap in the proof – in the proof of Lemma 2.8, a particular variety $I_{8}$ is claimed to be locally irreducible, because it is irreducible. This is false as it stands.

However, the author believes the result is still true, and that D'Souza's argument can be made to work.
