Example 5.4. (97DB)

Let $X_{6} \subset \P^{2}$ be a general plane sextic. Then the moduli map $\mu_{1}: \P^{2*} \dashrightarrow \m_{0,6}/S_{6}$ is generically injective. Since the target is three dimensional, we expect the double-point locus of $\mu_{1}$ to be a curve $Y \subset \P^{2*}$. Is this the case?

If so, the assignment $X \mapsto Y$ is a contra-invariant of ternary sextics – what are the order and degree of this contra-invariant?
