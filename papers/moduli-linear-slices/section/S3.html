Section 3. Grauert-Mulich

The goal of this section is to generalize the classical Grauert-Mulich theorem [OSS80, Theorem 2.1.4] in two directions:

Proposition 3.1. (6C21)

Let $Z\subset \mathbb{P}^N$ be a smooth projective variety and $f: \mathbb{P}^1\to Z$ be a general map in an open subset $\cM$ of $\operatorname{Mor}(\mathbb{P}^1, Z)$ such that $\PP^1\times \cM\to Z$ has connected fibers. Suppose $f^{*}T_Z$ is globally generated.

Let $E$ be a torsion free sheaf on $Z$ and write $f^{*}E$ as $\bigoplus_{i=1}^b \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1\geq \cdots \geq a_b$. If $a_j>a_{j+1}+1$ for some $i$, then $E$ has a subsheaf of rank $i$ and degree $\frac{1}{\deg(f)}\sum_{i=1}^ja_i$. In particular, if $E$ is semistable, then the bundle $f^{*}E$ can be written as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $|a_i - a_{i+1}| \leq 1$. \begin{comment} Let $Z$ be a smooth variety, $E$ be a vector bundle on $Z$ and $C\cong \mathbb{P}^1$ be a free curve on $Z$ that is general in its deformation class and satisfies that the space of curves in the deformation class of $C$ through a general point of $Z$ is connected.

Write the bundle $E|_C$ as $\bigoplus_{i=1}^b \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1\geq \cdots \geq a_b$. If $a_j>a_{j+1}+1$ for some $i$, then $E$ has a subsheaf of rank $i$ and degree $\frac{1}{\deg(C)}\sum_{i=1}^ja_i$. In particular, if $E$ is semistable, then the bundle $E|_C$ can be written as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $|a_i - a_{i+1}| \leq 1$. \end{comment}

For applications to slicing by $k$-planes, we will use Proposition 3.3 [5EF1].

Definition 3.2. (52AC)

Given a torsion free sheaf $E$ on a smooth projective variety, let $\mu^{\max}(E)$ denote the maximum slope of a nontrivial subsheaf of $E$. It is also the slope of the first subsheaf appearing in its Harder-Narasimhan filtration. Similarly let $\mu^{\min}(E)$ denote the minimum slope of a nontrivial quotient of $E$. It is also the slope of the quotient of the last two subsheaves appearing in its Harder-Narasimhan filtration.

Proposition 3.3. (5EF1)

Let $E$ be a torsion free sheaf on $\mathbb{P}^n$. Let $\Lambda$ be a general $k$-plane in $\mathbb{P}^n$. Let $S\subsetneq E|_{\Lambda}$ be a sheaf appearing in the Harder-Narasimhan filtration of $E|_{\Lambda}$ and suppose $$\begin{aligned}\mu^{\min}(S)-\frac{1}{k}>\mu^{\max}(E|_{\Lambda}/S),\end{aligned}$$ then $E$ is not semistable.

The proofs of Proposition 3.1 [6C21] and Proposition 3.3 [5EF1] are very similar in spirit to standard proofs of Grauert-Mulich, such as the one found in [OSS80]. The argument relies crucially on the following Lemma.

Lemma 3.4. (F582)

{OSS80}}]

Let $Y$ and $Z$ be smooth varieties and $\pi: Y\to Z$ be a surjective smooth morphism with connected fibers. Let $E$ be a vector bundle on $Z$ such that $\pi^{*}E$ has a vector subbundle $S$ with quotient vector bundle $Q$. Then, if $\Hom(T_{Y/Z},\Hom(S,Q))=0$, then $S$ is the pullback of a subbundle of $E$ on $Z$.

The key technical lemma of this section is Lemma 3.6 [6E5A], whose proof will use the following simple fact in Lemma 3.5 [3430].

Lemma 3.5. (3430)

Let $Y$ be a variety and $E$ and $F$ be two sheaves on $Y$. Suppose all the semistable subquotients in the Harder-Narasimhan filtration of $E$ have greater slope than the corresponding semistable subquotients of $F$. Then, $\Hom(E,F)=0$.

Proof. (7353)

Let $0=E_0\subset E_1\subset \cdots\subset E_a=E$ be the Harder-Narasimhan filtration for $E$ and $0=F_0\subset F_1\subset \cdots\subset F_b=F$ be the Harder-Narasimhan filtration for $F$. Consider a map $\phi: E\to F$. We show $\phi=0$.

First, $\phi$ induces a map $E_1\to F_b/F_{b-1}$, which is zero since the source is semistable and has slope greater than the target, which is also semistable. Therefore, $\phi$ induces a map $E_1\to F_{b-1}/F_{b-2}$, which again is zero for the same reason. Continuing this, we find the map $E_1\to F$ is zero.

Then, we consider the induced map $E_2/E_1\to F_{b}/F_{b-1}$ and repeat the argument above to find $E_2/E_1\to F$ must be the zero map. Continuing this for $E_3/E_2$ and so on shows that the map $\phi$ is zero.

Lemma 3.6. (6E5A)

Let $Z$ be a smooth projective variety and let $\cU \to \cM$ be a smooth family of projective varieties with a smooth surjective map $\pi:\cU \to Z$ having connected fibers. Let $E$ be a torsion free sheaf on $Z$ and let $\cU_p$ be a general fiber of $\cU\to \cM$. Let $S$ be a subsheaf of $\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}$ appearing in the Harder-Narasimhan filtration of $\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}$ such that $$\begin{aligned}\mu^{\min}(S)+\mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/Y}|_{\cU_p})>\mu^{\max}(\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S),\end{aligned}$$ then there is a subsheaf $\widetilde{S}$ on $Z$ of $E$ such that $\widetilde{S}|_{\cU_p}$ is a subsheaf of $\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}$ agreeing with $S$ on the locus where $S$ is a vector bundle.

Proof. (4D72)

By [Sha77, Lemmas 5 and 7], we can replace $\cM$ by a dense open subset so that the members of the Harder-Narasimham filtration of $\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}$ extend to a family over $\cU$. Namely, there exists a sequence of subsheaves $0=S_0\subset S_1\subset \cdots S_a= \pi^{*}E$ that restricts to the Harder-Narasimham filtration of $E|_{\cU_p}$ for all $p\in \cM$, so in particular $S=S_i|_{\cU_p}$ for some $i$.

We have an open subset $\cU^0\subset \cU$ whose complement has codimension at least 2 and consists of the points over which $S_i$ and $\pi^{*}E/S_i$ are both vector bundles. The image of $\cU^0$ in $Z$ is an open subset $Z^0$ (by flatness of $\pi$) whose complement must also have codimension at least 2.

Now, we can apply Lemma 3.4 [F582] in the case $Y=\cU^0$ and $Z=Z^0$. In order to do so, we must show that $$\begin{aligned}\Hom(T_{\cU^0/Z^0},\Hom(S_i|_{\cU^0},(\pi^{*}E/S_i)|_{\cU^0}))=0.\end{aligned}$$ For this, let $C \subset \cU_{p}$ be a general complete intersection curve of sufficiently high degree. Restricting the Harder-Narasimhan filtration of $\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}$ to $C$ will result in a sequence of vector sub-bundles. This is because each semistable subquotient on $\cU_{p}$ is in particular torsion-free, so the Harder-Narasimhan filtration is a sequence of vector subbundles away from a set of codimension at least 2. Since $C$ can be chosen to avoid this set of codimension 2, restricting a sequence of sub-bundles yields a sequence of sub-bundles. By [MR82], this sequence of sub vector bundles on $C$ is the Harder-Narasimhan filtration of $\pi^{*}E|_{C}$. $\Hom(T_{\cU/Z}|_{\cU_p}\otimes S,\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S)=0$. In order to show (3.1), it now suffices to show $$\begin{aligned}\Hom(T_{\cU/Z}|_{C},\Hom(S|_C,\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S)|_C))=\Hom(T_{\cU/Z}|_{C}\otimes S|_C,\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S)|_C)=0.\end{aligned}$$

We conclude by applying Lemma 3.5 [3430] as $$\begin{aligned}\mu^{\max}(\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S)|_C) &= \deg(C) \mu^{\max}(\pi^{*}E|_{\cU_p}/S))\\ \mu^{\min}(S|_C) &= \deg(C) \mu^{\min}(C)\\ \mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/Z}|_{C}) &= \deg(C) \mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/Z})\\ \mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/Z}|_{C}\otimes S|_{C}) &= \mu^{\min}(S|_C) + \mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/Z}|_{C}).\end{aligned}$$

In order for us to apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A], it is necessary to understand the sheaf $T_{\cU/Z}|_{\cU_{p}}$. The Lemma 3.8 [4668] and Lemma 3.9 [E1C1] identifies the sheaf in two common situations.

Definition 3.7. (1E5A)

Let $Y$ be a variety and $V$ be a globally generated vector bundle on $Y$. Then, the Lazarsfeld-Mukai bundle of $V$ is the kernel of the evaluation map $\OO_Y\otimes H^0(V)\to V$.

Lemma 3.8. (4668)

Let $Z$ be a smooth projective variety and $\cM$ be a smooth open subset of a component of the Hilbert scheme of varieties on $Z$. Let $\cU$ be the universal family, and suppose that the natural map $\pi: \cU \to Z$ is smooth. Let $\cU_{p}$ be a general fiber of $\cU \to \cM$. Then $T_{\cU/Z}|_{\cU_{p}}$ is the Lazarsfeld-Mukai bundle for the normal bundle $N_{\cU_{p}/Z}$, defined by the short exact sequence $$0 \to T_{\cU / Z}|_{\cU_{p}} \to H^0(N_{\cU_{p}/Z}) \otimes \cO_{\cU_{p}} \to N_{\cU_{p}/Z} \to 0.$$

Proof. (7526)

First we compare the normal sheaf of $\cU$ in $\cM \times Z$ to the normal sheaf $N_{\cU_p/Z}$. We have the following diagram.

Lemma 3.9. (E1C1)

Let $Y$ and $Z$ be smooth projective schemes and $\cM$ be an open subset of $\operatorname{Mor}(Y,Z)$. Let $\pi: Y\times\cM\to Z$ be the universal map. For $f: Y\to Z$ in $\cM$, suppose $f^{*}T_Z$ is globally generated. Then, the restriction $T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}$ is an extension of $T_Y$ by the Lazersfeld Mukai bundle of $f^{*}T_Z$.

Proof. (F8F0)

By definition, we have the short exact sequence $$\begin{aligned}0\to T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}\to T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}\to f^{*}T_Z\to 0.\end{aligned}$$ We have the natural decomposition $T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}\cong H^0(f^{*}T_Z)\otimes \cO\oplus T_Y$, with respect to which the natural map $T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}\to f^{*}T_Z$ is $\operatorname{ev}+df$. The rows and columns are exact and the left column gives $T_{Y\times\cM/Z}|_{Y\times\{[f]\}}$ as an extension of $K$ by $T_Y$.

Lemma 3.10. (FBF7)

The Lazarsfeld-Mukai bundle of any globally generated vector bundle on $\PP^1$ is a direct sum of $\cO(-1)$'s.

Proof. (3D2F)

Taking Lazarsfeld-Mukai bundles behaves well with respect to direct sum, so it remains to show the result for line bundles $\cO(a)$ with $a \geq 0$. The Lazarsfeld-Mukai bundle $M$ satisfies $$0 \to M \to \cO \otimes H^0(\cO(a)) \to \cO(a) \to 0 .$$ It follows that $M$ has rank $a$, degree $-a$ and no global sections, so that $M= \cO(-1)^a$. The result follows.

Proof of Theorem 3.1 [6C21]. (E509)

We apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] to our situation, where $\cM$ is an open subset of $\operatorname{Mor}(\mathbb{P}^1,Z)$ containing $[f]$ and $\cU=\PP^1\times \cM$. Then, applying Lemma 3.9 [E1C1] shows $T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}$ is the direct sum of the Lazersfeld Mukai bundle of $f^{*}T_Z$ with $T_{\PP^1}$. By Lemma 3.10 [FBF7], the Lazersfeld Mukai bundle of $f^{*}T_Z$ is a sum of $\OO(-1)$'s, so $T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}$ is an extension of $\OO(2)$ by a direct sum of $\OO(-1)$'s implying $\mu^{\min}(T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}}) \geq -1$.

Suppose $f^{*}E$ splits as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1 \geq \cdots \geq a_r$ and $a_j \leq a_{j+1}-2$. Letting $S=\oplus _{i\leq j}\cO(a_i)$, we find $$\begin{aligned}a_j + (-1) &>a_{j+1}\\ \mu^{\min}(S)+\mu^{\min}(T_{\PP^1\times\cM/Z}|_{\PP^1\times\{[f]\}})&>\mu^{\max}((f^{*}E)/S),\end{aligned}$$ Therefore, we can apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] and conclude. \begin{comment} Let $\cM$ be a smooth open subset of the Hilbert scheme of curves in $Z$ containing the class of $C$, and let $\cU \to \cM$ denote the universal family. Then the natural map $\pi: \cU \to X$ has connected fibers by hypothesis. We can pull back $E$ to $\cU$ and the restriction to the fibers of $\cU \to \cM$ will be the same as the restriction of $E$ to the corresponding curves.

Suppose $E|_C$ splits as $\bigoplus_i \cO(a_i)$ with $a_1 \geq \cdots \geq a_r$ and $a_j \leq a_{j+1}-2$. Letting $S=\oplus _{i\leq j}\cO(a_i)$, we find $$\begin{aligned}a_j + (-1) &>a_{j+1}\\ \mu^{\min}(S)+\mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/\cU_p}|_C)&>\mu^{\max}(E|_{C}/S),\end{aligned}$$ as $T_{\cU/\cU_p}|_C$ is a direct sum of $\cO(-1)$ by Lemma 3.10 [FBF7]. Therefore, we can apply Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] and conclude. \end{comment}

Proof of Proposition 3.3 [5EF1]. (7C8A)

This follows from Lemma 3.6 [6E5A] with $Z=\mathbb{P}^n$, $\cM=\mathbb{G}(k,n)$ and $\cU$ the universal $k$-plane. The only thing to check is $\mu^{\min}(T_{\cU/\mathbb{P}^n}|_\Lambda)=-\frac{1}{k}$. By Lemma 3.8 [4668], $T_{\cU/\mathbb{P}^n}|_\Lambda$ lies in the sequence $$\begin{aligned}0\to T_{\cU/\mathbb{P}^n}|_{\Lambda}\to H^0(\OO_{\Lambda}(1)^{n-k})\otimes \OO_{\Lambda}\to \OO_{\Lambda}(1)^{n-k} \to 0,\end{aligned}$$ and so is isomorphic to $\Omega_{\Lambda}(1)^{n-k}$ by the Euler sequence. Since $\Omega_{\Lambda}(1)$ is semistable with slope $-\frac{1}{k}$ [OSS80, Theorem 1.3.2], the result follows.
