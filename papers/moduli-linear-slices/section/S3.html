Section 3. Maximal monodromy

The purpose of this section is to establish a basic result about the monodromy of lines meeting a general hypersurface at the least possible number of points.

Theorem 3.1. (8CDE)

Suppose $k = d - (2r-2) \geq 2 $, and suppose $\sum_{i=1}^{k}m_{i}=d$ is a partition with at least two distinct parts. Then the Galois group of the generically finite projection $$\begin{aligned}\varphi : I_{k}\left( m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \right) \to \P^{N}\end{aligned}$$ is the full symmetric group.

Our approach to proving Theorem 3.1 [8CDE] is standard. Let $G$ denote the Galois group of $\varphi$. We break the proof up into two standard steps, in increasing difficulty:

  1. Show $G$ is $2$-transitive.
  2. Show $G$ contains a simple transposition.

These two properties imply that $G$ is the full symmetric group. Step $1$ is easy and quite standard, whereas Step $2$ requires some care, and will occupy most of our attention.
