4.3. Stable reduction

For the reader's convenience, we briefly review the procedure of stable reduction for families of $d$-pointed rational curves.

\begin{notation} \label{notation5} The following notation will appear frequently in this section:

\begin{enumerate} \item Let $B$ be a smooth curve, and $b \in B$ a specified point.

\item Let $\sigma_{1}, \dots, \sigma_{d}$ be distinct sections of the projection $\P^{1} \times B \to B$. We assume that away from $b$, the sections $\sigma_{i}$ are mutually disjoint. We let $\P^{1}_{b}$ denote $\P^{1} \times \left\{ b \right\}$.

\item Let $$\begin{aligned}D := \left( \bigcup_{i}\sigma_{i} \right) \cap \P^{1}_{b}.\end{aligned}$$ and write $D = p_{1} + \dots p_{n} + a_{1}q_{1} + \dots a_{k-n}q_{k-n}$, with $a_{i}>1$ and all points $p_{j}, q_{j}$ distinct.

\item We denote stable genus $0$ curves in the usual way as $(P,s_{1}, \dots, s_{d})$. The nodes and marked points of $(P,s_{1}, \dots, s_{d})$ are called special points. \end{enumerate} \end{notation}

Proposition 4.11. (86A6)

Assume $k \geq 3$. Then the stable replacement $\left( P, s_{1}, \dots , s_{d} \right) \in \barm_{0,d}$ of the pair $D \subset \P^{1}_{b}$ is a union of

  1. $\P^{1}_{b}$ and
  2. $T_{i}, i = 1, \dots k-n$, possibly-nodal rational tails with $T_{i} \cap \P^{1}_{b} = q_{i}.$
The marked points $\left\{ s_{1}, \dots, s_{d} \right\}$ are distributed as follows: on $\P^{1}_{b}$ the points $p_{1}, \dots, p_{n}$ are marked, while each $T_{i}$ contains $a_{i}$ marked points.

Proof. (1149)

We recall the stable reduction procedure, from which the proposition is immediately verified.

  1. We view $C := \bigcup \sigma_{i} \subset \P^{1} \times B$ as a curve on a surface, with singularities $q_{i} \in \P^{1}_{b}$.
  2. We resolve the singularities $q_{i} \in C$ by blowing up repeatedly until all branches of $C$ through $q_{i}$ are separated.
  3. Since each blow up in the resolution process happens at smooth centers, the new fiber $P$ of the blown up surface over the point $b \in B$ is the union of the original $\P^{1}_{b}$ and several rational tails attached to $\P^{1}_{b}$ at the points $q_{i}$.
  4. We contract any components of $P$ which have $\leq 2$ special points.

The proposition follows from this description of the stable reduction process.

Remark 4.12. (CE30)

The process of stable reduction described in the proof of Proposition 4.11 [86A6] will be referred to frequently in the remainder of this section. We emphasize that when $k \geq 3$, the original fiber $\P^{1}_{b}$ naturally persists as a component of the stable reduction $P$.

Definition 4.13. (EF09)

A chain is a nodal curve $T = P_{1} \cup \dots \cup P_{j}$ where each $P_{i} \equiv \P^{1}$, and $P_{i}$ intersects $P_{i+1}$ at one node, for all $i = 1, \dots j-1$, with no further intersections between components.

Proposition 4.14. (B98F)

Assume $(P,s_{1}, \dots , s_{d}) \in \barm_{0,d}$ is a stable curve which is the nodal union of:

  1. a $\P^{1}$ with $n$ marked points $p_{1}, \dots, p_{n}$, and
  2. $k-n$ chains $T_{i}$, $i = 1, \dots k-n$, with each $T_{i}$ intersecting $\P^{1}$ once at a point $q_{i}$, every component of $T_{i}$ having exactly three special points, and each $T_{i}$ containing $a_{i}>1$ marked points.
Suppose $(P, s_{1}, \dots, s_{d})$ is the stable replacement for $D \subset \P^{1}_{b}$ in Section 4.3.

Then

  1. $D \subset \P^{1}_{b}$ is supported on $\leq k$ points.
  2. If $D$ is supported on $k$ points, and if we assume $k \geq 4$, then $D$ must equal $p_{1}+ \dots + p_{n} + a_{1}q_{1} + \dots a_{k-n}q_{k-n}$.

Proof. (5C9C)

These two points follow from the stable reduction procedure described in the proof of Proposition 4.11 [86A6], and from the observations in Remark 4.12 [CE30].
