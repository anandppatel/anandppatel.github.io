2.1. Local models

Throughout this section $X \subset \P^{r}$ will be a smooth hypersurface not containing any lines.

Definition 2.1. (90B3)

We let $\pi : \P \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ denote the tautological $\P^{1}$-bundle.

Definition 2.2. (0924)

The point-line incidence correspondence for $X$ is the variety $\Sigma \subset \P$ defined by $$\begin{aligned}\Sigma := \left\{ (x, [\ell]) \in X \times \G\left( 1,r \right) \mid x \in \ell \right\}\end{aligned}$$ We let $\varphi, \pi$ be the projections of $\Sigma$ to the first and second factors.

Lemma 2.3. (669D)

$\Sigma$ is a smooth variety.

Proof. (5616)

The projection $\varphi : \Sigma \to X$ is a projective bundle, with fibers isomorphic to $\P^{r-1}$.

Clearly, since $X$ is assumed to contain no lines, the map $\pi : \Sigma \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ is a finite branched cover of degree $d$.

Definition 2.4. (BD96)

We let $W_{n}$, $(n > 1)$, denote the pair $(Y_{n},\P^{1} \times \A^{n-1} \to (\A^{n-1},0))$, with $Y_{n} \subset \P^{1} \times \A^{n-1}$ defined by the equation $$\begin{aligned}s^{n} + a_{n-2}s^{n-2}t^{2} + a_{n-3}s^{n-3}t^{3} + \dots + a_{0}t^{n} = 0,\end{aligned}$$ where $a_{i}$ are coordinates on $\A^{n-1}$, and $[s:t]$ are homogeneous coordinates on $\P^{1}$.

Remark 2.5. (FC31)

To simplify notation, we will often write the affine equation $$z^{n} + a_{n-2}z^{n-2} + \dots a_{0}= 0$$ for the equation defining $W_{n}$. $W_{n}$ is often referred to as the ``mini-versal unfolding'' of the singularity $z^{n} = 0$.

Proposition 2.6. (B813)

Let $B$ be a smooth variety, $b \in B$ a marked point. Suppose $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B$ is a divisor such that $Z_{b} \subset \P^{1} \times \left\{ b \right\}$ is a scheme isomorphic to $s^{n} = 0$. Then there is an étale neighborhood $U$ of $b$ and a map $\mu : \left( U,b \right) \to \left( \A^{n-1},0 \right)$ such that $Z_{U} \subset \P^{1}\times U$ is isomorphic to the pullback of $W_{n}$ under $\mu$.

Proof. (4D72)

Since $B$ is smooth, it is étale locally isomorphic to $\A^{m}.$ So we may reduce to the case $\left( B,b \right) = \left( \A^{m},0 \right)$.

After passing to an open set, the set $Z$ is defined by a single equation $$s^{n} + u_{n-1}s^{n-1}t + \dots + u_{0}t^{n}=0$$ where $u_{i} \in \fm_{0} \subset \Gamma (O_{\A^{m}})$. (Here $\fm_{0}$ is the maximal ideal of the origin.) We may ``complete the $n$-th power'' and find an isomorphic $Z' \subset \P^{1} \times \A^{m}$ defined by a similar equation, but with vanishing $u_{n-1}$ term.

The $n-1$ functions $u_{i} \in \fm_{0} \subset \Gamma \left( O_{\A^{m}} \right), i = 0, \dots n-2$ define a map $\left( \A^{m}, 0 \right) \to \left( \A^{n-1},0 \right)$ with the desired property.

Proposition 2.7. (110D)

Let $(B.b)$ be as in the previous proposition, but now suppose $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B$ is a divisor whose restriction to $Z_{b} \subset \P^{1} \times \left\{ b \right\}$ is of the form $n_{1}p_{1} + \dots + n_{i}p_{i}$ with each $n_{j} \geq 1$.

Then there exists an étale open neighborhood $U$ of $b$ such that $Z_{U}$ is a disjoint union $ \coprod_{j}Z_{j} $, and furthermore there exists a map $(U,b) \to \left( \prod_{j}\A^{n_{j}-1},0 \right)$ such that $Z_{j}$ is isomorphic to the pullback of $W_{n_{j}}$ under the composite $(U,b) \to \left(\prod_{j}\A^{n_{j}-1},0 \right) \to \left(\A^{n_{j}-1},0\right)$.

Proof. (9F85)

The argument is completely similar to the proof of Proposition 2.6 [B813].

Remark 2.8. (CCC4)

The maps to $W_{n}$ in these propositions are not unique, however it is easy to show that they induce a unique map on the tangent space of $B$ at $b$.

Definition 2.9. (11DD)

Let $(B,b)$ be a smooth variety with marked point, and let $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B$ be a closed subset such that $Z_{b}$ is as in Proposition 2.7 [110D]. Then $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B \to B$ is versal at $b$ if any (and hence all) of the maps $(U,b) \to \left(\prod_{j}\A^{n_{j}-1},0 \right)$ is a local submersion, i.e. the map on tangent spaces is surjective.

Definition 2.10. (8657)

Let $(B,b)$ be a smooth variety with marked point, and let $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B$ be a closed subset such that $Z_{b}$ is as in Proposition 2.7 [110D]. Then $Z \subset \P^{1} \times B$ is mini-versal at $b$ if any (and hence all) of the maps $(U,b) \to \left(\prod_{j}\A^{n_{j}-1},0 \right)$ is a local isomorphism.
