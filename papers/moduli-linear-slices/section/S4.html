Section 4. Extending $\mu_{1}$ across a $k$-incident line

\begin{notation}\label{notation4} Throughout this section:

  1. $X \subset \P^{r}$ is a general hypersurface of degree $d$.
  2. We set $k := d - (2r-2)$, and assume $k \geq 1$.
  3. We let $\pi : \Sigma \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ be the point-line correspondence for $X$.
  4. We continue to let $$\begin{aligned}\mu_{1}: \G\left( 1,r \right) \dashrightarrow \Hyp(d,1)\end{aligned}$$ denote the moduli map.
  5. Finally, we let $\ell$ be a $k$-incident line of $X$ with multiplicity $\left( m_{1}, \dots, m_{k} \right)$.
\end{notation}

Remark 4.1. (8537)

We will assume that the reader has some familiarity with the moduli space $\barm_{0,d}$. We will review some important background as appropriate.

Our goal is to understand the resolution of indeterminacy of $\mu_{1}$ near $\ell$.

The moduli space $\Hyp(d,1)$ is none other than the space $\m_{0,d}/S_{d}$. Unfortunately the latter moduli space is not fine, so we will first change settings to circumvent this technical inconvenience.

Definition 4.2. (7975)

We define $$\begin{aligned}\Sigma^{\left[ d \right]} := \overline{\Sigma \times_{\pi} \dots \times_{\pi}\Sigma \setminus \text{Diagonals}}\end{aligned}$$ We let $s: \Sigma^{[d]} \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ denote the structural degree $d!$ map.

Proposition 4.3. (F8EA)

The scheme $\Sigma^{[d]}$ is smooth at any point $\hat{[\ell]} \in s^{-1}([\ell])$.

Proof. (0486)

This is a consequence of Corollary 2.26 [BC02]. Indeed, étale locally around every $[\ell] \in \G\left( 1,r \right)$, we may assume $\pi : \Sigma \to \G\left( 1,r \right)$ is isomorphic to products of maps of the form $$\begin{aligned}\left\{ (z,a_{0}, \dots ,a_{n-2}) \mid z^{n} + a_{n-2}z^{n-2} + \dots a_{0}=0 \right\} \to (a_{0}, \dots ,a_{n-2})\end{aligned}$$ If $r_{1}, \dots r_{n}$ denote the roots of $z^{n} + a_{n-2}z^{n-2} + \dots + a_{0}$, then the variety $$\Spec k\left[ r_{1}, \dots r_{n}\right]/(r_{1}+\dots+ r_{n}=0)$$ maps to $\Spec k\left[ a_{n-2}, \dots , a_{0}, \right]$ by the elementary symmetric expressions in the $r_{i}$.

This, in turn, means that the scheme $\Sigma^{\left[ d \right]}$, above the point $\left[ \ell \right] \in \G\left( 1,r \right)$, is étale locally isomorphic to a product of affine spaces $\Spec k[r_{1}, \dots, r_{n}]/(r_{1} + \dots + r_{n})$, and in particular smooth.

Lemma 4.4. (ADE2)

The set $s^{-1}([\ell])$ has $\frac{d!}{m_{1}!m_{2}! \dots m_{k}!}$ elements.

Proof. (0395)

The group $S_{d}$ acts transitively on the set $s^{-1}([\ell])$. From the local description of $s$ over $[\ell]$ explained in the proof of the previous proposition, we see that the stabilizer of a point in $s^{-1}([\ell])$ is a copy of $S_{m_{1}} \times \dots \times S_{m_{k}} \subset S_{d}$.

Definition 4.5. (3519)

We let $\pi^{\left[ d \right]} : \P \to \Sigma^{\left[ d \right]}$ denote the tautological $\P^{1}$-bundle, pulled back from $\G\left( 1,r \right)$. $\pi^{\left[ d \right]}$ has $d$ tautological sections $\sigma_{i}$, $i = 1, \dots d$.

We let $U \subset \Sigma^{\left[ d \right]}$ denote the open set over which none of the sections $\sigma_{i}$ intersect one another. We obtain an induced moduli map which we write as: $$\begin{aligned}\mu_{1}^{\left[ d \right]} : U \to \barm_{0,d}.\end{aligned}$$

Finally, we let $\hat{[\ell]}$ be any point in $\Sigma^{[d]}$ lying over $[\ell]$.
