Proof. (5C7F)

Given a matroid $\MM$ with $n$ elements in its groundset, recall the independence polytope $I_\MM\subset\mb{R}^n$ is given by $$I_\MM=\bigcap_{A \subset \{1,\ldots,n\}}\{\sum_A e_i \le \rk(A)\}\cap \bigcap_{i \in \{1,\ldots,n\}}\{e_i \ge 0\}$$ and the rank polytope $P_\MM$ is given by $$P_\MM=I_\MM \cap \{\sum x_i=\rk(\MM)\}.$$ The truncation $\tau^{\le l}\MM$ has independence polytope $I_{\MM} \cap \{\sum x_i\le l\}$ and rank polytope $I_{\MM}\cap \{\sum x_i=l\}$. A singleton $\ast$ of rank 1 has $I_\ast=[0,1]$. The independence polytope of $\MM_1 \cup \MM_2$ is given by $I_{\MM_1 \times \MM_2}=I_{\MM_1}\times I_{\MM_2}$.

Finally, we recall the polytope description of series and parallel connection. Let $P_\MM$ have coordinates $x_1,\ldots,x_m,x$, and $P_\NN$ have coordinates $y,y_1,\ldots,y_n$. Then if we project $P_\MM \times P_\NN$ under the map $$\operatorname{Add}:\mb{R}^m\times \mb{R}\times \mb{R}\times \mb{R}^n \to \mb{R}^m\times \mb{R}\times \mb{R}^n$$ induced by the addition map $\mb{R}\times \mb{R} \to \mb{R}$, then this is injective as $P_\MM$ and $P_\NN$ lie in sum of coordinate hyperplanes. Call the new $\mb{R}$-coordinate $\bar{xy}$ and the associated unit vector $e_{\bar{xy}}$. Then it is known that $$\begin{aligned}&P_{S(\MM,\NN)}=\operatorname{Add}(P_\MM\times P_\NN \cap x+y \le 1)\\ &P_{P(\MM,\NN)}=\operatorname{Add}(P_\MM\times P_\NN \cap x+y \ge 1)-e_{\bar{xy}}.\end{aligned}$$

Consider the projection $\phi:I_{\MM \cup \NN}=I_\MM \times I_\NN \to \mb{R}^2$ given by $(\sum x_i,\sum y_i)$. Let the coordinates on $\mb{R}^2$ be $(s,t)$. Then the preimages $$J_{ab}=\phi^{-1}([a,a+1]\times [b,b+1]\cap \{k-1 \le s+t \le k\})$$ for $a+b\in \{d-1,d\}$ with $a,b \ge 0$ form a subdivision of $$J=\phi^{-1}(\{k-1 \le s+t \le k\})\subset I_{\MM \cup \NN}.$$ We make the following claims. $$\begin{aligned}\pi_{\bar{xy}}(\tau^{\le d+1}(\MM \cup \NN \cup \ast))=&J\\ \pi_{\bar{xy}}(P(\tau^{\le (a+1)}\MM,\tau^{\le (b+1)}\NN))=&J_{ab}\text{ when }a+b=d\text{ and }a,b\ge 0\\ \pi_{\bar{xy}}(S(\tau^{\le (a+1)}\MM,\tau^{\le (b+1)}\NN))=&J_{ab}\text{ when }a+b=d-1\text{ and }a,b\ge 0\end{aligned}$$ Because rank polytopes lie in a sum of coordinates hyperplane, we can reconstruct any coordinate from the identity of the others, so $\pi_{\bar{xy}}$ is injective on each of the above polytopes and the corresponding subdivision of $J$ induces the subdivision we are trying to prove.

First, we show $\pi_{\bar{xy}}(\tau^{\le k+1}(\MM \cup \NN \cup \ast))=J.$ Indeed, by our descriptions of rank polytopes of truncations and independence polytopes of unions, we get $$\begin{aligned}\pi_{\bar{xy}}(\tau^{\le k+1}(\MM \cup \NN \cup \ast))=&\pi_{\bar{xy}}(I_{\MM \cup \NN} \times [0,1] \cap \{\sum x_i+\sum y_i + \bar{xy}=k+1\})\\ &=I_{\MM \cup \NN} \cap \{k \le \sum x_i+\sum y_i \le k+1\}=J.\end{aligned}$$ Next, we show $\pi_{\bar{xy}}(P(\tau^{\le (a+1)}(\MM\cup \ast),\tau^{\le (b+1)}(\NN\cup \ast)))=J_{ab}\text{ when }a+b=k-1\text{ and }a,b\ge 0.$ Indeed, by our description of parallel connection and truncations of polytopes, we have $$\begin{aligned}&\pi_{\bar{xy}}(P(\tau^{\le (a+1)}(\MM \cup \ast),\tau^{\le (b+1)}(\NN\cup \ast)))\\ =&\pi_{\bar{xy}}(\operatorname{Add}(I_\MM\cap \{\sum x_i+x = a+1\} \times I_\NN \cap \{\sum y_i+y=b+1)\cap \{x+y \ge 1\})\\\intertext{and since $x+y \ge 1$ is equivalent to $\sum x_i+\sum y_i \le a+b+1=k+1$, this is} =&I_\MM\times I_\NN \cap \left(\{\sum x_i \in [a,a+1]\}\times \{\sum y_i \in [b,b+1]\}\right)\cap \{\sum x_i+\sum y_i \ge k+1\}=J_{ab}.\end{aligned}$$ Finally, we show $\pi_{\bar{xy}}(S(\tau^{\le (a+1)}\MM,\tau^{\le (b+1)}\NN))=J_{ab}\text{ when }a+b=k-1\text{ and }a,b\ge 0.$ Indeed, we have $$\begin{aligned}&\pi_{\bar{xy}}(S(\tau^{\le (a+1)}(\MM \cup \ast),\tau^{\le (b+1)}(\NN\cup \ast)))\\ =&\pi_{\bar{xy}}(\operatorname{Add}(I_\MM\cap \{\sum x_i+x = a+1\} \times I_\NN \cap \{\sum y_i+y=b+1)\cap \{x+y \le 1\})\\\intertext{and since $x+y \le 1$ is equivalent to $\sum x_i+\sum y_i \ge a+b+1=k$, this is} =&I_\MM\times I_\NN \cap \left(\{\sum x_i \in [a,a+1]\}\times \{\sum y_i \in [b,b+1]\}\right)\cap \{\sum x_i+\sum y_i \ge k\}=J_{ab}.\end{aligned}$$
