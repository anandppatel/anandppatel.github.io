Proof. (32B3)

Let $V=K^{r+1}$. Then, the canonical inclusion of $\mc{S}\subset V\times \mb{G}(1,V)$ of vector bundles over $\mb{G}(1,V)$ induces a surjection ${\rm Sym}^d(V^\vee)\times \mb{G}(1,V)\to{\rm Sym}^d(\mc{S}^\vee)$ of vector bundles. The section $\mb{G}(1,V)\to {\rm Sym}^d\mc{S}^\vee$ is the composition $$\begin{aligned}\mb{G}(1,V)\to {\rm Sym}^d(V^\vee)\times \mb{G}(1,V)\to {\rm Sym}^d\mc{S}^\vee.\end{aligned}$$ If $X=V(f)$ contains no lines, then $f$ restricts to something nonzero on each line, so $\mb{G}(1,V)\to {\rm Sym}^d\mc{S}^\vee$ avoids zero. The formula for $[\im(s)]$ follows from [EH16, Proposition 9.13].
