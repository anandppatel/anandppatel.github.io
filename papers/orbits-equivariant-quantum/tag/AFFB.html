Remark 3.9. (AFFB)

From this, we can express the $GL_{r+1}$-equivariant class $[\overline{\Gamma_M}]$ in terms of the classes $\opc{\tau^{\le k+1}(M \oplus \ast)}$ used to define $[M]_{\hbar}$ (see Theorem 11.1 [A77E]). This expression in turn may be seen as an application of Brion's formula applied to $(r+\frac{1}{n})(1,\ldots,1) - (r+1)I_M$ with $H$ used non-equivariantly as a grading variable to ensure the expression is homogenous of rank $rn$, see the proof of Theorem 10.4 [25D7].
