Proof of Lemma 7.9 [6FEC]. (4730)

Define the linear map $\phi : \mb{R}^n \times \mb{R} \to \mb{R}^n \times \mb{R}$ by $\phi(x, \lambda) = (x + \lambda v, \lambda)$ and define $Q = \phi(P \times \mb{R}_{\geq 0})$. Note that $(y,\lambda) \in Q$ if and only if $\lambda \ge 0$ and $y-\lambda v \in P$. Then, for any $x \in P + \mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$, the minimum of $\{\lambda \mid (x, \lambda) \in Q\}$ is exactly $h(x)$. Hence, the lower convex hull of $Q$ is $P^h$, so the facets of the regular subdivision of $P + \mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$ induced by $h$ are the projections of the lower facets of $Q$ to $P + \mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$.

Because $\phi$ is a linear embedding, the facets of $Q$ are $\phi(P \times \{0\})$ and $\phi(F \times \mb{R}_{\geq 0})$ for the facets $F$ of $P$. The facet $\phi(P \times \{0\})$ is a lower facet and $\phi(F \times \mb{R}_{\geq 0})$ is a lower facet if and only if $F$ is a shadow facet. Projecting these facets to $P + \mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$ yields the shadow facet subdivision. \begin{comment} Let $\mc{P}$ be the collection of polytopes containing $P$ and all polytopes $F+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v$, where $F$ ranges over all shadow facets.

First, note that the condition on $F$ being a shadow facet is equivalent to $N_F \cdot v >0$, where $N_F$ is the outward normal vector to the facet.

Suppose $z \in (P+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v) \setminus P$, and let $\pi(z)$ be its projection in the $-v$-direction onto $P$. We claim at least one facet containing $\pi(z)$ is a shadow facet. Otherwise, the inequalities locally defining $P$ around $z$ are $N_F \cdot x \ge C_F$, where $N_F \cdot v \le 0$ and $F$ ranges over facets containing $z$. Thus, we can add $-\epsilon v$ to $\pi(z)$ for small $\epsilon>0$ without leaving the polytope $P$, contradicting the definition of $\pi(z)$. Hence, we get that the polytopes in $\mc{P}$ cover $P+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v$.

Define $h_F: P+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v\to \mb{R}$ by $$\begin{aligned}h_F(z)&= \frac{N_F\cdot z-C_F}{N_F\cdot v},\end{aligned}$$ where $N_F$ is the outward pointing normal vector to a shadow facet $F\subset P$ and $C_F$ is such that $F=\{N_F\cdot x=C_F\}\cap P$. Equivalently, $h_F(z)$ is the value of $\lambda$ such that $z-\lambda v \in {\rm Span}(F)$.

We claim that for $z\in P+\mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$ that $$\begin{aligned}h(z) &= \max(\max_{F\text{ shadow facet}}{h_F(z)},0).\end{aligned}$$ This also shows $h$ is convex. Because the polytopes in $\mc{P}$ cover $P+\mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$, we have $$\begin{aligned}h(z) &= \begin{cases} 0&\qquad\text{if $z\in P$}\\ h_F(z) &\qquad\text{if $z\notin P$ and $\pi(z)\in F$ for $F$ a shadow facet.} \end{cases}\end{aligned}$$ Therefore, we clearly have $$\begin{aligned}h(z) &\leq \max(\max_{F\text{ shadow facet}}{h_F(z)},0).\end{aligned}$$ To show the other inequality, it suffices to show $h_F(z)\leq h(z)$ for all shadow facets $F$. This is true for $z\in P$ since in this case $h_F(z)\leq 0$. If $z\in (P+\mb{P}_{\geq 0}v)\backslash P$, then for a shadow facet $F$, we clearly have $h_F(z)\leq h(z)$, since $z-h_F(z)v \in \{N_F\cdot x= C_F\}$, $z-h(z)v\in P\subset \{N_F\cdot x\le C_F\}$, and $N_F \cdot v >0$.

To finish, it suffices to show that $P=\{z|\ h(z)=0\}$ and $F+\mb{R}_{\geq 0}v=\{z|\ h(z)=h_F(z)\}$ for all shadow facets $F$. The first equality is trivial and $\{z|\ h(z)=h_F(z)\}\supset F+\mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$. For the other containment, suppose $h(z)=h_F(z)$. Since $z-h(z)v\in P$ and $z-h_F(z)v\in {\rm Span}(F)$, we know $z-h(z)v\in P\cap {\rm Span}(F)=F$, so $z\in F+\mb{R}_{\geq 0}v$.
