Proof of Lemma 7.6 [6299]. (7870)

Let $C'$ be the nonnegative span of $\{e_i - e_j \in C \mid i \not \in v, j \in v\}$. By Lemma 14.6 [7C5A], we have $(v+C) \cap \Delta = (v+C') \cap \Delta$. Hence, by replacing $C$ with $C'$, we may assume that if $e_i - e_j \in C$ for $i \neq j$, then $i \not \in v$ and $j \in v$. Also, by multiplying $M$ and $M'$ on the left by elements of $GL_d$, we may assume that $M^v=(M')^v=I$.

Consider the matrix $M(t) = tM + (1-t)M'$. We claim that for general $t$, we have $P_{M(t)} = P_M = P_{M'}$. By specializing $t \to 1$, we have $P_M \subset P_{M(t)}$. It remains to show that $P_{M(t)} \subset P_{M}$.

Let $A$ be the matrix such that $A^v = I$ and all other entries of $A$ are zero. Define $B$ as in Proposition 7.8 [795C], and let $N = A(1+tB)$. Then $N^v = I$ and by Proposition 7.8 [795C], we have $P_N = (v+C) \cap \Delta = P_M = P_{M'}$. (Here, we use the assumption that if $e_i - e_j \in C$ for $i \neq j$, then $i \not \in v$ and $j \in v$.) Because $M^v = I$, the linear dependencies between a column of $M$ outside of $M^v$ with columns of $M$ inside $M^v$ determine the locations of the non-zero entries of $M$. Similar considerations apply for $N$ and $M'$. As $M$, $M'$, and $N$ have the same matroid, they have nonzero entries in the same locations. Since the nonzero entries of $N$ outside $N^v$ are general, we conclude that $M(t)$ is formed by specializing the general entries of $N$. Thus $P_{M(t)} \subset P_N = P_M$, as desired.

In Theorem 6.6 [F6CF], choose $D = \mb{A}^1_t$ and $M_D(t) = M(t)$. By choosing $U \subset \mb{P}^{(r+1)\times d - 1} \times (D \setminus \{0, 1\})$, we obtain a family $\wt{\mc{O}} = \overline{\psi(U)}$ that is flat over both $0 \in D$ and $1 \in D$. Part (a) of the lemma follows.

By Lemma 6.16 [453E], we have $\opc{M} = \opc{M_D(t)}$ for general $t$. For the same reason $\opc{M'} = \opc{M_D(t)}$, which proves (b).
