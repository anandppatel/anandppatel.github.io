Proof of Theorem 7.1 [4218]. (AF51)

Let $M$ be a $d \times n$ rank $d$ matrix such that $\dim P_M=n-1$. The construction in [DF10, Appendix A] yields an algorithm for writing $1_{P_M}=\sum \epsilon_i 1_{v_i+C_i}$, where the $C_i$ are either strictly positive matroidal cones or positive matroidal cones of dimension less than $n-1$, $v_i \in \Vt(P_M)$, and $\epsilon_i=\pm 1$ (repetition may occur). Each step of the algorithm replaces an indicator function $1_P$ with $P$ some possibly unbounded polytope with $1_{P+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v}-\sum_F 1_{F+\mb{R}_{\ge 0}v}$, where the sum ranges over the shadow facets $F$ of $P$ associated to $v=e_i-e_j$ with $i>j$ in the affine span of $P$, and no indicator functions are ever combined (even if they are identical to each other with opposite signs).

Starting with the admissible pair $(M,\{0\})$, we apply each step of the algorithm in conjunction with Theorem 7.12 [76CA], discarding any indicator functions which are not of full dimension in $\{\sum x_i=d\}$ (importantly we do not discard indicator functions just because the intersection with $\Delta$ is not of full dimension). This yields matrices $N_i$, strictly positive matroidal cones $C_i$, $v_i \in \Vt(P_M)$, and $\epsilon_i=\pm 1$ with $1_{P_M}-\sum \epsilon_i 1_{v_i+C_i}$ supported on a codimension $\ge 1$ subset of $\{\sum x_i=d\}$, $(N_i,C_i)$ admissible pairs with $P_{N_i}+C_i=v_i+C_i$, and $\opc{M}=\sum \epsilon_i \opc{N_i}$ (here we used Corollary 6.12 [95CF] to guarantee what we discarded away contributed zero to the equality of equivariant Chow classes).

Suppose now we have $\sum a_i \langle M_i \rangle =0$, and $P_{M_i}$ is of full dimension in $\Delta$ for all $i$. Then we get strictly positive matroidal cones $C_i$, $v_i \in \Vt(\Delta)$, and $\epsilon_i=\pm 1$ with $\sum a_i 1_{P_{M_i}}-\sum \epsilon_i 1_{v_i+C_i}$ supported on a codimension $\ge 1$ subset of $\{\sum x_i=d\}$, and admissible pairs $(N_i,C_i)$ with $P_{N_i}+C_i=v_i+C_i$ and $\sum a_i \opc{M_i}=\sum \epsilon_i \opc{N_i}$. But $\sum a_i 1_{P_{M_i}}$ is supported on a codimension $\ge 1$ subset of $\Delta$, so $\sum \epsilon_i 1_{v_i+C_i}$ is supported on a codimension $\ge 1$ subset of $\{\sum x_i=d\}$.

We claim that for each $1_{v_i+C_i}$ if we add its coefficients in the sum we get zero. This proves the result as by Lemma 7.6 [6299], all $\opc{N_i}$ associated to these terms are equal. Indeed, by the positivity, any cone which intersects a small neighborhood of the lexicographically minimal apex $v$ has its apex at $v$, so if we restrict to cones with apex at $v$, the sum is still supported on a codimension $\ge 1$ subset of $\{\sum x_i=d\}$. But by the strict positivity, intersecting all such cones with the plane $x_n=v_n+1$ yields a combination of strictly positive matroidal cones in one dimension less, and we may use induction to show the result is true for these cones. Removing these cones and repeating, we get the result.
