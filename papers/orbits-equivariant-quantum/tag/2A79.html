Theorem 3.1. (2A79)

Let $M$ be a $d \times n$ matrix with nonzero columns $x_1,\ldots, x_n$. If $\rk(M) < d$, then $\op{M} = 0$. Otherwise, for each permutation $\sigma\in S_n$, let $B(\sigma)$ be the lexicographically first $d$-element subset $\{i_1,\ldots, i_d\} \subset \{1,\ldots,n\}$ with respect to the ordering $\sigma(1)\prec \cdots\prec\sigma(n)$ such that $x_{i_1},\ldots,x_{i_d}$ is a basis of $K^d$. Then for indeterminates $z_1,\ldots,z_n$, the expression $$\sum_{\sigma \in S_n} \left(\prod_{i \in \{1,\ldots,n\}\setminus B(\sigma)} F(z_i)\right)\frac{1}{(z_{\sigma(2)}-z_{\sigma(1)})\ldots (z_{\sigma(n)}-z_{\sigma(n-1)})}$$ is a polynomial of degree at most $r$ in each $z_i$ with coefficients in $A^{\bullet}_{GL_{r+1}}(\pt)$, and the equivariant Chow class $\opc{M}$ is given by evaluating this polynomial at $H_1,\ldots,H_n$.
