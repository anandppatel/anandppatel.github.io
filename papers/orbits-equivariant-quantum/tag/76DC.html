Remark 4.3. (76DC)

It is not necessary to use the full strength of Theorem 7.1 [4218] to specialize to a rational normal curve. Rather, we can instead see directly what happens in the flat limit of orbits when we specialize. To see that no other top dimensional components appear in the flat limit, we may use that the non-equivariant class computed in [Li17, Theorem 1.1] (see Theorem 5.2 [48AB]) doesn't change when we specialize.

In the case $d=r+1$, we already know how the general orbit specializes from the literature [Kap93, Proposition 1.2.11, Proposition 1.2.15].
