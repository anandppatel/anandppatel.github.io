Section 4. Motivating example and the connection of $\mu_M$ with $QH_{GL_{r+1}}^\bullet(\mb{P}^r)$

Here we make precise the connection between generalized matrix orbits and the small equivariant quantum cohomology of $\mb{P}^r$, assuming the part of Theorem 3.2 [F804] asserting that $\opc{M}$ depends only on the matroid of $M$. In this section only, we will assume $\operatorname{char} K = 0$.

The concrete focus of this section will be on the following problem.

Problem 4.1. (418E)

What is the class in $A^{\bullet}_{GL_{r+1}}((\mb{P}^r)^n)$ of the closure of the $GL_{r+1}$-orbit of $(p_1,\ldots,p_n)\in (\mb{P}^r)^n$, where $p_1, \ldots, p_n \in \mb{P}^r$ are general? (The expression for the class of this orbit closure computed in [BF17, Theorem 5.1] is incorrect because their expression for ``$[Y_{\pi(v)}]_T$'' does not satisfy the degree hypothesis of [BF17, Theorem 3.5 (ii)].)

Let $M_{r+1}$ be an $(r+1) \times n$ matrix whose projectivized columns are these general $p_1, \ldots, p_n$. By Proposition 2.11 [B259] the $GL_{r+1}$-orbit closure of $(p_1,\ldots, p_n)$ is $\op{M}$ if $n > r+1$ and $(\mb{P}^r)^n$ if $n \le r+1$.

Problem 4.2. (7D2C)

If $M_{d+1}$ is a general $(d+1)\times n$ matrix, what is $\opc{M_{d+1}}\in A^{\bullet}_{GL_{r+1}}((\mb{P}^r)^n)$? \begin{comment} Given $n$ general points $p_1,\ldots,p_n$ in $\mb{P}^d$, we can consider the locus in $(\mb{P}^r)^n$ that is the image of the ``orbit map'' $$\begin{aligned}\mu: \mb{P}^{(r+1)d-1}\dashrightarrow (\mb{P}^r)^n,\end{aligned}$$ where $\mb{P}^{(r+1)d-1}$ is viewed as the projectivized space of $(r+1) \times d$ matrices and $\mu$ sends the class of a matrix $A$ to $A$ evaluated on each $p_i$. What is the equivariant class of the image of the orbit map, or, more precisely, what is the pushforward of the fundamental class after resolving $\mu$? \end{comment}

We will assume $n\geq d+2$ for the remainder of Section 4.
