Section 12. Application: line sections of a hypersurface

We consider the following problem.

Problem 12.1. (307B)

For a general hypersurface $X\subset\mb{P}^r$ of degree $d$ with $d=2r+1$, what is the degree of the map $$\begin{aligned}\phi_X: \mb{G}(1,r)\dashrightarrow {\rm Sym}^d\mb{P}^1//SL_2\cong \ol{\mc{M}_{0,d}}/S_d\end{aligned}$$ that sends each line $\ell\subset \mb{P}^r$ to the moduli of the $d$ unordered points $\ell \cap X$ on $\ell\cong \mb{P}^1$?

When $r=2$, the answer to Problem 12.1 [307B] was shown to be 420 in [CL08]. We give a separate approach that yields the answer for all $r$. More generally, in the threshold $d\geq 2r-2$ where a general such $X$ contains no lines, we find the class of a general fiber of $\phi_X$ in $\mb{G}(1,r)$.

We can also ask a related question.

Problem 12.2. (9EA7)

With the setup of Problem 12.1 [307B], when $r=2$ Cadman and Laza [CL08] show $$\begin{aligned}\deg(\phi_X)= 2\cdot (\text{\# bitangents of $X$})+4\cdot (\text{\# flexes of $X$}) = 2\cdot 120 + 4\cdot 45 = 420.\end{aligned}$$ How does this generalize to arbitrary $r$?

We solve Problem 12.1 [307B] in Theorem 12.5 [49A2] and answer Problem 12.2 [9EA7] in Section 12.3.
