2.2. Nodal curves and inflations of the normal bundle

A common setting for inflations in the paper is the following. Let $V$ be a smooth variety. Let $X$ and $R$ be curves in $V$ that intersect at a point $p$ so that their union $Z$ has a node at $p$. In particular, $X$ and $R$ are smooth at $p$. Also assume that $X$ and $R$ are local complete intersections elsewhere.

In local coordinates, the setup looks as follows. Let $t_1, \dots, t_d$ be formal local coordinates around $p$ in $V$. Let the curve $Z$ be cut out by $t_1t_2 = t_3 = \dots = t_d = 0$, the curve $X$ by $t_2 = t_3 = \dots = t_d = 0$, and the curve $R$ by $t_1 = t_3 = \dots = t_d = 0$.

In this situation, we get the exact sequence $$0 \to I_{Z/V} \big|_X \to I_{X/V}\big|_X \to \Omega_R \big|_p \to 0,$$ where the map $I_{X/V}|_X \to \Omega_R|_p$ is induced by the composite of $d \from I_{X/V} \to \Omega_V|_X$ and the restriction $\Omega_V|_X \to \Omega_V|_p \to \Omega_R|_p$. The dual sequence $$0 \to N_{X/V} \to N_{Z/V}\big|_X \to \shExt^1_{\O_X}\left(\Omega_R\big|_p, \O_X\right) \to 0,$$ exhibits $N_{Z/V}|_X$ as a degree 1 inflation of $N_{X/V}$ at $p$. The defining quotient of this degree 1 inflation $$q \from I_{X/V}\big|_p \to \Omega_{R}\big|_p$$ is given by the composite of $d \from I_{X/V} \to \Omega_V$ and the restriction $\Omega_V|_p \to \Omega_R|_p$. In terms of the local coordinates above, $q$ sends $t_2$ to $dt_2$ and $t_3, \dots, t_d$ to $0$. If the image of $T_p R$ in $N_{X/V}|_p$ is a general one-dimensional subspace, then $N_{Z/V}|_X$ is a general degree 1 inflation of $N_{X/V}$ at $p$.

Observe that we have natural identifications $$\begin{aligned}\shExt_{\O_X}^1\left(\Omega_R\big|_p, \O_X\right) &= \shExt_{\O_X}^1\left(\k_p, \O_X\right) \otimes N_{p/R} \\ &= N_{p/X} \otimes N_{p/R}.\end{aligned}$$ Using this identification, we can write the sequence (2.3) and its analogue on $R$ together as

In local coordinates, the map above can be described as follows. We have the $k$-vector spaces $$\begin{aligned}N^\vee_{Z/V}|_p &= I_{Z/V}/I_{Z/V}^2|_p = \langle t_1t_2, t_3, \dots, t_d \rangle\\ N^\vee_{p/R}|_p &= I_{p/R}/I_{p/R}^2|_p = \langle t_1 \rangle, \text{ and }\\ N^\vee_{p/X}|_p &= I_{p/X}/I_{p/X}^2|_p = \langle t_2 \rangle.\end{aligned}$$ The map in (2.6) is dual to the map $$t_1 \otimes t_2 \mapsto t_1t_2.$$

Finally, note that the discussion above extends naturally to the case of two smooth curves attached nodally at a finite set of points instead of a single point.
