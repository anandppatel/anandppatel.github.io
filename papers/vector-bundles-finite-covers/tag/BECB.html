Proof. (BECB)

The restriction map $\Sym^* F^\vee \to \phi_*\O_X = \O_Y \oplus E^\vee$ induces a map $$\lambda \from F^\vee \to E^\vee.$$ Since a general fiber $X_y \subset F_y$ is in affine general position, the map $\lambda$ is an injective map of sheaves. But the source and the target are locally free of the same degree and rank. Therefore, $\lambda$ is an isomorphism.

Recall that the affine canonical embedding is induced by the map $$(0, \id) \from E^\vee \to \O_Y \oplus E^\vee = \phi_* \O_X.$$ Suppose $\iota$ induces the map $$(\alpha, \lambda) \from F^\vee \to \O_Y \oplus E^\vee.$$ Compose $\iota$ with the affine linear isomorphism of $T_\alpha \from \Tot(F) \to \Tot(F)$ over $Y$ defined by the map $\Sym^*F^\vee \to \Sym^*F^\vee$ induced by $$(-\alpha, \id) \from F^\vee \to \O_Y \oplus F^\vee.$$ Then $T_\alpha \circ \iota \from X \to F$ is the affine canonical embedding, as desired.
