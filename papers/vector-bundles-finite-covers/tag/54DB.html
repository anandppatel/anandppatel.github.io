Proof. (54DB)

First, note that $N_{Z/\widetilde{P}}\big|_R$ is a vector bundle of rank $(d-1)$ and degree $(d-2)(d+1)+1$. The map $N_{R_y/P_y} \to N_{Z/\widetilde{P}}\big|_{R_y}$ is the composite $$N_{R_y/P_y} \to N_{R_y/\widetilde{P}} \to N_{Z/\widetilde{P}}\big|_{R_y}$$ Using that $X$ is transverse to $P_y$, a local computation shows that the injection $N_{R_y/P_y} \to N_{Z/\widetilde{P}}\big|_{R_y}$ remains an injection when restricted to any point of $R_y$. Since $R_y \subset P_y \cong \P^{d-1}$ is a rational normal curve, we know that $N_{R_y/P_y} \cong \O(d+1)^{d-2}$ (see, for example, [29, II] or [31, Example 4.6.6]). We thus get an exact sequence $$0 \to \O(d+1)^{d-2} \to N_{Z/\widetilde{P}}\big|_{R_y} \to \O(1) \to 0.$$ Since $\Ext^1(\O(1), \O(d+1)) = 0$, this sequence splits, and we get the desired isomorphism.
