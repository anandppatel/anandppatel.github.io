2.3. Divisors from the algebra of covers

Just as we get special loci in $\widetilde H_{d,g}$ from non-generic topological behavior, we get special loci in $\widetilde H_{d,g}$ from non-generic algebraic behavior. We make this precise using a structure theorem for finite morphisms due to Casnati and Ekedahl [4], which we first recall.

Let $X$ and $Y$ be integral schemes and $\alpha \from X \to Y$ a finite flat Gorenstein morphism of degree $d \geq 3$. The map $\alpha$ gives an exact sequence $$0 \to \O_Y \to \alpha_* \O_X \to {E_\alpha}^\vee \to 0,$$ where $E = E_\alpha$ is a vector bundle of rank $(d-1)$ on $Y$, called the Tschirnhausen bundle of $\alpha$. Denote by $\omega_\alpha$ the dualizing sheaf of $\alpha$. Applying $\Hom_Y(-,\O_Y)$ to (2.1), we get $$0 \to E \to \alpha_* \omega_\alpha \to \O_Y \to 0.$$ The map $E \to \alpha_* \omega_\alpha$ induces a map $\alpha^* E \to \omega_\alpha$.

Theorem 2.3. (F1CE)

[See {[4, Theorem 2.1]}]

In the above setup, $\alpha^* E \to \omega_\alpha$ gives an embedding $\iota \from X \to \P E$ with $\alpha = \pi \circ \iota$, where $\pi \from \P E \to Y$ is the projection. Moreover, the following hold.

  1. The resolution of $\O_X$ as an $\O_{\P E}$ module has the form $$\begin{split} 0 \to \pi^* N_{d-2} (-d) \to \pi^* N_{d-3}(-d+2) \to \pi^*N_{d-4}(-d+3) \to \dots \\ \dots \to \pi^*N_2(-3) \to \pi^*N_1(-2) \to \O_{\P E} \to \O_X \to 0, \end{split}$$ where the $N_i$ are vector bundles on $Y$. Restricted to a point $y \in Y$, this sequence is the minimal free resolution of $X_y \subset \P E_y$.
  2. The ranks of the $N_i$ are given by $$\rk N_i = \frac{i(d-2-i)}{d-1} {d \choose {i+1}},$$
  3. We have $N_{d-2} \cong \pi^* \det E$. Furthermore, the resolution is symmetric, that is, isomorphic to the resolution obtained by applying $\Hom_{\O_{\P E}}(-, N_{d-2}(-d))$.

We call the resolution in (1) the Casnati–Ekedahl resolution of $\alpha$.

Let us take $Y = \P^1$. Every vector bundle on $\P^1$ splits as a direct sum of line bundles. The multi-set of degrees of the line bundles appearing in the direct sum decomposition is unique. We refer to this multi-set as the splitting type of the bundle. We say that a bundle $V$ is balanced if the splitting type is $\{a,\dots, a\}$ for some $a$.

Proposition 2.4. (AB0E)

Let $\alpha \from C \to \P^1$ be a point of $\widetilde H_{d,g}$. Denote by $E$ the Tschirnhausen bundle and by $N_i$ the syzygy bundles in the Casnati–Ekedahl resolution of $\alpha$. Then $$\begin{aligned}\deg E &= (g+d-1), \text{ and }\\ \deg N_i &= (d-2-i) (g+d-1) {{d-2} \choose i-1}.\end{aligned}$$

Proof. (4698)

The branch divisor of $\alpha$ is cut out by a section of $(\det E)^{\otimes 2}$. Therefore, we get $2 \deg E = 2g+2d-2$, from which the first equation follows. We postpone the proof of the second equation to Section 4 (See Corollary 4.3 [BFA5]).

Suppose $d$ divides $g-1$. Then the rank of $N_i$ divides its degree.

Proposition 2.5. (D380)

If $d$ divides $g-1$, then for a generic $\alpha \from C \to \P^1$ in $\widetilde H_{d,g}$ and $i = 1, \dots, d-2$, the bundle $N_i$ is balanced.

We postpone the proof to Section 3.

Definition 2.6. (C934)

Suppose $d$ divides $g-1$. Define the $i$th syzygy divisor $\mu_i \subset \widetilde H_{d,g}$ as the locus of $\alpha \from C \to \P^1$ for which the bundle $N_i$ is unbalanced.

There is a natural scheme structure on $\mu_i \subset \widetilde {\orb H}_{d,g}$, defined as follows. Let $U \to \widetilde{\orb H}_{d,g}$ be an étale local chart for the moduli stack over which the conic bundle $\orb P_U \to U$ admits a relative $\O(1)$. Consider the bundle $\End(N_i) \otimes \O(-1)$ on $\orb P_U$. Note that $\chi \left(\End(N_i) \otimes \O(-1)\right) = 0$ and $h^1(\End(N_i) \otimes \O(-1) \geq 1$ if and only if $N_i$ is unbalanced. The divisor $\mu_i$ is the zero locus of the first Fitting ideal of $R^1p_* \left(\End(N_i) \otimes \O(-1) \right)$. Henceforth, $\mu_i$ is understood to have this scheme structure.

Remark 2.7. (2483)

We can use the splitting types of $E$ and $N_i$ to define locally closed subsets of $\widetilde H_{d,g}$ of higher codimensions. Doing so gives a stratification of $\widetilde H_{d,g}$ according to the isomorphism types of the bundles appearing in the Casnati–Ekedahl resolution. This stratification has a distinctly algebro-geometric favor, and it should be in some sense orthogonal to the topological stratification discussed in Remark 2.2 [9145]. See [17] for more on this stratification.
