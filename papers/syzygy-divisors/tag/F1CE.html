Theorem 2.3. (F1CE)

[See {[4, Theorem 2.1]}]

In the above setup, $\alpha^* E \to \omega_\alpha$ gives an embedding $\iota \from X \to \P E$ with $\alpha = \pi \circ \iota$, where $\pi \from \P E \to Y$ is the projection. Moreover, the following hold.

  1. The resolution of $\O_X$ as an $\O_{\P E}$ module has the form $$\begin{split} 0 \to \pi^* N_{d-2} (-d) \to \pi^* N_{d-3}(-d+2) \to \pi^*N_{d-4}(-d+3) \to \dots \\ \dots \to \pi^*N_2(-3) \to \pi^*N_1(-2) \to \O_{\P E} \to \O_X \to 0, \end{split}$$ where the $N_i$ are vector bundles on $Y$. Restricted to a point $y \in Y$, this sequence is the minimal free resolution of $X_y \subset \P E_y$.
  2. The ranks of the $N_i$ are given by $$\rk N_i = \frac{i(d-2-i)}{d-1} {d \choose {i+1}},$$
  3. We have $N_{d-2} \cong \pi^* \det E$. Furthermore, the resolution is symmetric, that is, isomorphic to the resolution obtained by applying $\Hom_{\O_{\P E}}(-, N_{d-2}(-d))$.
